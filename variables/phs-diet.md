# PHS Diet (Primary Health System)

The Primary Health System (PHS) is a component of the Variables layer in [[Human Design System]]. It describes the optimal way each person's body takes in food and information. PHS is derived from the tone (sub-color) of the Design Sun/Earth position in the chart.

## Core Principle

Ra Uru Hu taught that the body's digestion system is individual and specific. What is optimal for one person's body may be counterproductive for another's. The six PHS types describe different modes of engagement with food, environment, and sensory input that best support each person's physical health and cognitive clarity.

"The body has its own intelligence about how to be fed correctly. That intelligence is not the same for everyone." - Ra Uru Hu (paraphrased)

PHS is considered an advanced topic, best explored after establishing a foundation in type, strategy, authority, and profile.

## The Six PHS Types

### 1. Appetite
The person eats when they have genuine appetite -- not at scheduled times, not socially, but only when the body produces genuine hunger. Eating without real appetite diminishes digestion and health for this type.

### 2. Taste
The person's digestion is guided by taste and flavor. Specific tastes (sweet, salty, bitter, sour, pungent, astringent) signal what the body needs. Eating should follow genuine taste sensation rather than nutritional logic alone.

### 3. Thirst
The person's optimal intake is organized around thirst rather than structured eating. Drinking (including broths, soups, and liquid foods) is prioritized over solid food as the primary mode of nourishment.

### 4. Touch
How food is touched, held, and prepared matters. The person's relationship to food is tactile -- the texture, temperature, and physical qualities of food affect digestion and absorption. They may benefit from knowing how their food was prepared and by whom.

### 5. Sound
The sonic environment during eating affects digestion. This type is sensitive to noise and conversation during meals. Eating in silence or with pleasant, calm sound supports optimal digestion; eating in chaotic or stressful environments impairs it.

### 6. Light
The amount and quality of light during eating and food preparation matters. This type is affected by whether they eat in full light or dim light, natural or artificial light. Their digestion is linked to their visual environment.

## Hot/Cold Variation

Each of the six PHS types also has a hot or cold variation, indicating whether the person's approach to eating (and information intake) is more active/direct (hot) or more passive/receptive (cold). This gives 12 distinct PHS sub-types.

## Information Diet

PHS does not apply only to food. It also describes how the body best takes in information, stimulation, and environmental input. A person with Appetite PHS benefits from consuming information only when genuinely hungry for it; a person with Sound PHS processes information better in particular sonic environments.

## PHS and Health

Ra Uru Hu positioned PHS as one of the most direct paths to physical health improvement. People who align their eating habits with their PHS type often report improved digestion, increased energy, and greater physical clarity -- independent of specific dietary content.
