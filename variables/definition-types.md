# Definition Types

Definition in [[Human Design System]] refers to how the defined (colored) centers in a person's BodyGraph are connected to each other. The pattern of connectivity divides the population into four definition types, each with distinct characteristics for how consistent energy flows and how much a person is affected by bridging conditioning from others.

## Single Definition

Approximately 41 percent of the population has Single Definition -- all of their defined centers are connected in one continuous circuit. There are no gaps or splits in the definition.

Single Definition people experience themselves as internally consistent and self-contained. Their energy flows smoothly through all their defined centers without depending on others to "bridge" any splits. They tend to be independent, to have a consistent sense of self, and to be less affected by the absence of others.

The challenge for Single Definition people can be a tendency toward inflexibility -- their consistency is genuine but can sometimes miss the value of outside perspectives.

## Split Definition

Approximately 46 percent of the population has Split Definition -- their defined centers form two separate circuits that are not directly connected to each other. These two groups of defined centers are called "splits."

Split Definition people experience a rhythm of wholeness when they encounter people (or transits) who bridge their splits, and a sense of incompleteness when they are alone. They may unconsciously seek out people who bridge their specific split, feeling more at ease, more complete, or more energized in those people's presence.

This creates vulnerability: a Split Definition person can be conditioned by the person who consistently bridges their split, associating completeness with that person's presence rather than with their own authority.

Understanding one's splits helps identify what is sought in others -- and to what degree that seeking is appropriate vs. conditioning-based.

## Triple Split Definition

Approximately 11 percent of the population has Triple Split Definition -- three separate circuits of definition, requiring three different kinds of bridging to feel connected. Triple splits benefit from diverse social environments and multiple social contexts to activate all three circuits.

Triple Split people are generally advised to make decisions after having been exposed to a variety of environments and people, since they need more social input to feel settled. They should not make major decisions in isolation.

## Quadruple Split Definition

Approximately 1 percent of the population has Quadruple Split -- four separate circuits of definition. This is rare and produces a person whose different areas of definition are almost never all bridged simultaneously. Quadruple Splits benefit from extensive exposure to diverse people and environments before settling into major decisions.

## Definition and Conditioning

The more splits a person has, the more their sense of wholeness is influenced by who is around them. This increases susceptibility to conditioning but also increases the richness of the social field they need to operate well.

[[Reflector]] types have no definition at all -- every center is open and undefined. They are entirely shaped by their environment, which is why correct environment and community are so central to Reflector wellbeing.
