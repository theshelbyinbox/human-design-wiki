# Channel 37/40 — The Channel of Community

The Channel of Community connects Gate 37 in the Solar Plexus to Gate 40 in the Heart (Ego) Center. It is the channel of the bargain — the deal struck between a community and its members about what is given and what is received. Ra Uru Hu described the 37/40 as the channel through which the basic infrastructure of human society has been organized. The marriage contract, the family bond, the work agreement, the loyalty between members of a tribe — all of it is written through this channel. People with the 37/40 defined are wired to be at the center of community life, brokering the bargains that hold the group together.

This channel sits in the Ego sub-circuit of the Tribal circuit, and it is one of the three channels in the BodyGraph that sit in opposition in the wheel — meaning it is very commonly defined by the Sun/Earth axis or the nodal axis. Ra noted that during the Cross of Planning, which has dominated humanity since 1610 and ends in 2027, the 37/40 has been the background frequency holding tribal society together. As that cycle closes, the influence of this channel as a cultural force is fading, even though the channel itself continues to operate in every chart that carries it.

## Mechanics

The Channel of Community connects the [[Solar Plexus Center]] (emotional awareness, feelings, the wave) to the [[Heart Center]] (will, ego, courage, material). Gate 37 sits in the Solar Plexus end; Gate 40 sits in the Heart end. Both gates active means both centers are defined, and the channel guarantees willpower in the chart — a continuous flow of emotional bonding meeting the will to provide.

The channel belongs to the Ego sub-circuit of the Tribal circuit. The Ego circuit includes 21/45 (Money), 26/44 (Surrender), 40/37 (Community), and 19/49 (Synthesis). Together these channels carry the tribal infrastructure — leadership, resources, bargains, and bonds.

If one person carries Gate 37 and another carries Gate 40, the electromagnetic completion of the channel produces a strong tribal pull between them, typically around themes of agreement, provision, and the question of who is going to take care of whom.

## The Gates

### Gate 37 — The Family (Friendship)

Gate 37 sits in the Solar Plexus Center as the Gate of Friendship and is described as the "gate of the mouth" — an odd designation for a Solar Plexus gate, but metaphorical. The 37 is the gate of affection. Ra was specific: "Not particularly or necessarily love, and not particularly or necessarily friendship, but affection — and this is a great massage that the 37 always provides the 40."

Affection, in Ra's reading, is the most profound glue of the tribe. It is what the 37 offers in exchange for what the 40 provides. People with Gate 37 have the ability to hold community together with their nurturing nature and to seek harmony as the source of outer peace. The challenge of Gate 37 is to find inner peace as the source of outer peace, rather than to over-attach to people and things as a way of feeling safe.

Repressed, Gate 37 over-attaches. Reactive, Gate 37 projects fears onto others and can struggle with taking advantage of others. The corrected expression is the warmth and affection that holds a community together honestly.

### Gate 40 — Deliverance (Aloneness)

Gate 40 sits in the Heart Center as the Gate of Deliverance, and equally the Gate of Aloneness. It is an Ego Tempering Gate — the will or lack of will to provide. People with Gate 40 are wired to be the breadwinner, to provide for their community, and to deliver. Because the energy is so often pouring outward, a sense of loneliness can build up. Retreat is necessary. Without it, the 40 burns out and isolates.

Ra called Gate 40 one of the two gates of denial in the BodyGraph (along with Gate 26). The 40 says, "I don't need you, because the moment you come near me I know what that means — I'm going to have to work." The bargain it strikes is the heart of the 37/40: the 37 offers affection, and the 40 agrees to work in exchange. "Okay, okay, okay I will, I'll take out the garbage because otherwise you're not going to give me any affection." — Ra Uru Hu

Repressed, Gate 40 has no boundaries and over-extends. Reactive, Gate 40 isolates and cuts off. The corrected expression is the will to provide, with healthy retreat built in.

## What This Channel Brings

The gift of Channel 37/40 is the ability to broker the bargains that build community. The person with this channel is naturally placed at the center of family, friend group, or workplace, mediating the exchange of provision and affection, work and care, contribution and belonging. When this channel is in correctness, the bargains it brokers are explicit, honest, and renegotiable. The community it holds together is a real community — a group bound by genuine agreements, not by guilt or obligation.

This is the channel that produces what Ra called the basic infrastructure of society. The marriage contract is written here. The family obligation is written here. The shared meal at the table, the mutual protection, the agreement that members of the tribe will take care of each other — all of it is the work of the 37/40. When this channel is defined and lived correctly, the person carries the warmth that holds a small society together.

The 40/37 also guarantees willpower in the chart, which means the person has the consistent capacity to provide. They are wired to work — and they are equally wired to need rest and affection in exchange. The bargain is built into the wiring.

## The Shadow / Not-Self Expression

The shadow of the 37/40 is the bargain that has gone unspoken or unhonored. The not-self 37/40 makes deals that are not explicit, expects affection that has not been earned, demands work that has not been agreed to, and feels chronically resentful when the unspoken contract is not honored. Family systems are particularly prone to this distortion: the obligation to provide and the expectation of loyalty become tangled with guilt, and the bargain becomes coercive rather than honest.

When the 37 is in its not-self, the person uses affection as a tool to extract work, or withholds affection as punishment. When the 40 is in its not-self, the person provides resentfully and then isolates, or refuses to retreat and burns out. The result is a community that feels like obligation rather than belonging.

Ra also placed the larger cultural shadow of this channel inside the breakdown of the family that has accelerated since 1961, when the retrograde of the Cross of Planning began. As the background frequency of the 37/40 fades, the bargains that have held human society together are renegotiating themselves at an unprecedented rate. "More attorneys and lawyers and barristers on this planet than in the entire history of the planet before 1961." The not-self 37/40 cannot find its way through this change, and produces broken contracts, broken bonds, and broken communities.

## In Relationship — The Electromagnetic Connection

When one person carries Gate 37 and another carries Gate 40, the electromagnetic completion is often felt as a strong family-style pull. The Gate 37 person feels the Gate 40 person's will to provide as exactly what their affection has been seeking. The Gate 40 person feels the Gate 37 person's warmth as exactly what their work has been working for. The connection produces a temporary experience of a complete tribal bargain.

In long-term partnership, this dynamic plays out around the question of who provides what. The bargain needs to be explicit. When it is unspoken, the relationship slowly becomes the not-self version of the channel — affection used to extract work, work performed to extract affection, both sides resentful.

## Circuit Context

The Tribal circuit is about support, survival of the lineage, and the bonds that hold a tribe together. The Ego sub-circuit specifically is the willpower and material infrastructure of the tribe. The 37/40 is the bargain channel of this sub-circuit. The 21/45 is the material leadership channel. The 26/44 is the surrender and salesmanship channel. The 19/49 is the synthesis channel of needs and loyalty.

Ra placed the 37/40 alongside the 19/49 as the deeply social channels of the tribe — and warned that both are tied to the breakdown of the Cross of Planning. As we move into the era of the 55-59, the tribal bargain begins to lose its background support. The 37/40 will continue to operate in every chart that carries it, but the social structure built around it is in transition. Correct expression — explicit bargains, honest affection, healthy retreat — becomes more important as the cultural scaffolding falls away.
