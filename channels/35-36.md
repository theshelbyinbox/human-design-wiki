# Channel 35/36 — The Channel of Transitoriness

The Channel of Transitoriness is the channel of change, of experience, of the jack-of-all-trades who must taste everything because nothing yet has been enough. It runs from the Solar Plexus into the Throat, carrying the emotional heat of inexperience up into expression as restless appetite for the next experience. This is the channel of the perpetual learner, the crisis manager, the one who has been there and done that — and still feels the pull to do it again, differently, with new eyes.

The Rave I'Ching calls this archetype the bufon de mil caminos, the fool of a thousand miles. The phrase appears across cultures because the energy is universal: the human being who cannot stop having experiences, who treats life as a continuous succession of arcs to be lived through, who rises from the ash of each crisis and immediately walks into the next one. This is the channel that, more than any other, carries the cost and the gift of the abstract way — the cost being chronic dissatisfaction, the gift being a life so deeply lived that nothing remains untouched by experience.

## Mechanics

Channel 35/36 connects the Solar Plexus Center (Gate 36, Crisis / The Darkening of the Light) with the Throat Center (Gate 35, Change / Progress). When both gates are defined, both centers are defined through this channel, and the person carries an emotionally motorized stream that drives experience all the way through to throat expression. This is one of the channels of talent — specifically, the talent for managing life crises and for the sexual hunger that drives much of the experiential process.

Because the Solar Plexus is the motor, this channel runs on the emotional wave. People with this channel defined have emotional authority. There is no clarity in the moment of desire; clarity arrives only after the wave has run its course.

The circuit is the Collective Abstract sub-circuit — the circuit of past experience, recall, and meaning-making after the fact.

## The Gates

### Gate 36 — Crisis / The Darkening of the Light (Solar Plexus)

Gate 36 in the Solar Plexus is, in Ra's framing, not primarily the gate of crisis — it is the gate of inexperience. The hexagram is The Darkening of the Light, and the action of the gate is the penetration of a surface that has never been crossed before. Gate 36 is what makes a person willing to try a thing they have never tried, to step into a situation they have no preparation for, to penetrate into experience they have not yet earned.

"The 36th gate is the gate of depth. People always think the 36th gate is the gate of crisis. It is one of its keys. It is, in fact, the gate of inexperience." — Ra Uru Hu

"This is an incredible power, this power to penetrate the surface, to break through, to go from inexperienced to experience." — Ra Uru Hu

Crisis is the byproduct, not the purpose. When an inexperienced being penetrates into a new situation, things often go badly the first time. The crisis is the educational mechanism. Lived correctly, the person walks through the crisis, learns what the experience actually was, and emerges with depth they did not previously have. Lived through the not-self mind, the same gate becomes a serial crisis-generator — drama for the sake of drama, novelty for the sake of escape.

A significant portion of Gate 36's experiential field is sexual. Sexual inexperience drives much of the early crisis the gate produces, and sexual experience is often where the gate finds its eventual mastery.

### Gate 35 — Change / Progress (Throat)

Gate 35 in the Throat is the gate that expresses change as throat-level articulation: "I have done this. I have been there. I want to do it again, but differently." The hexagram is Progress, and the emotional engine here is the throat-level voice of someone who has tasted and now wants more. Gate 35 is the expression of the experiential life — the speaking, the storytelling, the active throat that turns lived experience into something shareable.

Gate 35 is also the fuel gauge pointing to full. Where Gate 41 in the Root points to empty and drives the initial pressure for experience, Gate 35 sits at the top of the experiential stream and reports the moment of completion: I have had this experience; the tank is full; the wave is cresting. And immediately the gauge will reset. The abstract is never satisfied. The completion of one experience is the prompt for the next.

"Expectation is something that is always, always painful. It's painful. And it drives this whole cycle." — Ra Uru Hu

## What This Channel Brings

Channel 35/36 brings the talent for lived experience and the talent for crisis management. The defined person is here to live, taste, try, and rise from the inevitable crises with depth and articulation intact. This is the channel of the polymath, the person who has done many things and can talk about all of them — not because they wanted to be impressive but because the channel mechanically requires them to keep moving from one experience to the next.

The talent for crisis is real and load-bearing. People with this channel defined become, often without trying, the person others call in a crisis. They know what crises feel like from the inside; they have walked through enough of them to recognize the shape; they can guide others through the dark patch back to the surface. This is not a gentle gift, but it is a profound one. The phoenix-from-the-ashes archetype lives here.

The talent for sexual experience is also part of this channel's specific gift. Lived correctly through Strategy and Emotional Authority, the 35/36 person becomes capable of a depth of sexual experience and articulation that is unusual in human beings. The channel produces what is sometimes called the sex god or sex goddess archetype — not as a performance but as the natural result of a person who has lived sexual experience deeply and metabolized it.

The communication gift is significant. Because the channel runs into the Throat, the experiences are articulable. The 35/36 person can tell the story, name the feeling, share the lesson. This is the abstract way returning to the collective in the form of speech — experience converted into wisdom and offered to the rest of us.

## The Shadow / Not-Self Expression

The shadow of Channel 35/36 is chasing experience as escape. The Gate 36 pressure for novelty, ungoverned by Authority, produces a life of restless jumping — from job to job, partner to partner, place to place, never staying long enough to actually digest what an experience was. The mind, sensing the emptiness of each just-completed cycle, immediately reaches for the next thing. Decades pass; experiences accumulate; depth does not.

The second shadow is drama generation. Because the channel knows the texture of crisis intimately, it can also manufacture crisis when none is needed. The not-self 35/36 turns small inconveniences into existential disasters, finds the dramatic angle in every situation, and unconsciously generates the emotional intensity the channel has come to need. Other people experience this as exhausting; the person carrying it experiences it as life.

The third shadow is expectation. "The dilemma for the 35 is that the expectation is never fulfilled." — Ra Uru Hu. The not-self mind generates expectations of what each new experience will deliver, and the experience never matches the expectation. The result is chronic disappointment, the felt sense that nothing is ever quite right, that satisfaction is always one experience away. The correction is to live the experience without the expectation — to walk through, body present, wave riding, and let the recognition come at the end.

## In Relationship — The Electromagnetic Connection

When one person carries Gate 36 and another carries Gate 35, the electromagnetic connection produces a relationship of shared experiential intensity. The 36 brings the pressure to penetrate into the new; the 35 brings the throat-level expression of what change is supposed to feel like. Together they will live through many phases, many crises, many sexual and emotional intensities. The pull is hot and the wave is real. Both parties have to honor emotional authority or the relationship becomes a cycle of crisis-and-resolution that exhausts everyone involved.

## Circuit Context

The 35/36 sits in the Collective Abstract sub-circuit and represents the throat-level expression of the experiential way. Where the 30/41 starts the experiential cycle in the Root, the 35/36 completes it in the Throat — experience that has been lived and is now being articulated. The Abstract circuit accumulates wisdom through cycles of experience, and the 35/36 is where that wisdom finally becomes speech.

"You have that in the 35/36, this Channel of Transition, of change. It is inherent in the abstract process. When you see the abstract process mentally, you see ideas that come and go, ideas that have a beginning, a middle, and an end." — Ra Uru Hu

This is the circuit's way of teaching the species. One person lives the experience, walks through the crisis, rises from the ash, and tells the rest of us what they learned. The 35/36 is the throat of that telling.
