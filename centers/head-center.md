# Head Center

The Head Center (also called the Crown Center) is located at the top of the [[Human Design System]] BodyGraph. It is a pressure center that generates the drive to think, to understand, and to make sense of existence. Biologically associated with the pineal gland and the hypothalamus.

## Function

The Head generates inspiration and mental pressure. It poses questions and creates the compulsion to find answers. The pressure of the Head Center is not itself a problem -- it is the engine of human intellectual and spiritual inquiry. The problem arises when the mind treats this pressure as a command to resolve every question it generates, or when it uses the pressure to drive decisions.

The Head Center is not a motor. It cannot manifest action directly. Its energy flows downward into the [[Ajna Center]] for processing and conceptualization.

## Defined Head Center

A defined Head Center provides a consistent, fixed pattern of inspiration. People with a defined Head are consistently drawn to specific kinds of questions or ways of thinking. The inspiration is reliable and recognizable. Approximately 30 to 35 percent of people have a defined Head Center.

## Undefined Head Center

People with an undefined Head amplify the mental pressure and inspiration of defined Head people around them. Common not-self pattern: feeling pressure to think about and solve questions that are not theirs to resolve -- absorbing the mental agenda of others and treating it as their own.

"Are these questions that I'm obsessing about even mine? Or did I pick them up from someone else?" is a useful inquiry for an undefined Head Center person.

The wisdom of an undefined Head is the ability to entertain many different kinds of questions and inspirations without being attached to any of them. The undefined Head can be a broad receiver of inspiration without being driven by any single source.
