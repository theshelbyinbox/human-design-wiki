# Nine Centers

The Nine Centers are the geometric shapes that form the core of the [[Human Design System]] BodyGraph. They correspond loosely to the Hindu chakra system but differ in number, function, and arrangement. Human Design holds that humanity shifted from a seven-center configuration to a nine-center configuration in 1781, with the addition of the [[Ajna Center]] and [[Solar Plexus Center]] as new awareness centers.

## Overview

The nine centers are arranged in a diamond-shaped diagram. Each center governs specific themes, biological correlates, and functions. Centers connect to each other through 36 channels; the channels are composed of paired gates drawn from the 64 hexagrams of the I Ching.

Each center is either defined (colored) or undefined (white) in a person's chart. Defined centers generate consistent energy and represent fixed, reliable qualities. Undefined centers receive and amplify energy from others and the planetary transit field.

## The Nine Centers

### Head Center (Crown)
Located at the top of the BodyGraph. A pressure center. Governs inspiration, questions, and the drive to make sense of existence. Biologically associated with the pineal gland. The Head generates mental pressure -- the compulsion to think, to understand, to answer. This pressure is not the mind's authority; it is simply the engine of mental activity.

### Ajna Center
Located below the Head, connected to it and to the Throat. An awareness center. Governs conceptualization, mental processing, and the formation of opinions and beliefs. Biologically associated with the pituitary gland and prefrontal cortex. The Ajna takes inspiration from the Head and processes it into concepts. A defined Ajna has a consistent, fixed way of thinking; an undefined Ajna thinks in many different ways depending on conditioning.

### Throat Center
Located in the upper center of the BodyGraph. Governs manifestation and communication. The only center that has no direct equivalent in the traditional chakra system (as a motor/manifestation center). All energy in the BodyGraph eventually seeks expression through the Throat. When a motor center is directly connected to the Throat, the type becomes a [[Manifestor]] or [[Manifesting Generator]].

### G Center (Identity / Self)
Located in the center of the BodyGraph, shaped like a diamond. Governs identity, love, and direction in life. Contains the magnetic monopole, described as the force that holds together the two crystals of consciousness (personality and design) within the body. Biologically associated with the blood and liver. A defined G Center gives a consistent sense of identity and direction; an undefined G Center seeks identity through environment and relationships.

### Heart Center (Ego / Will)
Located to the upper right of the G Center. A motor center. Governs willpower, ego, the material world, promises, and self-worth. Biologically associated with the heart, stomach, and gall bladder. Only about 30 percent of people have a defined Heart Center. When defined, it provides consistent willpower but also carries the pressure to prove, compete, and achieve in material terms.

### Solar Plexus Center
Located to the right of the BodyGraph. Both a motor and an awareness center -- the only center that is both. Governs emotions, feelings, and the wave pattern of emotional experience. Biologically associated with the kidneys, pancreas, and lungs. Defined in approximately 50 percent of people. The Solar Plexus is described as the center of a future awareness mutation -- the emerging emotional consciousness of humanity.

### Sacral Center
Located in the lower center of the BodyGraph. A motor center. Governs life force, sexuality, fertility, creativity, and work capacity. Biologically associated with the sexual organs and digestive system. Defined only in [[Generator]] and [[Manifesting Generator]] types (approximately 70 percent of people). The Sacral is described as the most powerful motor in the BodyGraph and the source of the generative energy that sustains life on the planet.

### Splenic Center
Located to the left of the BodyGraph. An awareness center. Governs intuition, instinct, immune function, and in-the-now awareness. Biologically associated with the spleen, lymphatic system, and T cells. The oldest awareness center in evolutionary terms, carrying the accumulated survival wisdom of the species. Contains seven fear gates, each associated with a specific survival concern.

### Root Center
Located at the bottom of the BodyGraph. A motor and pressure center. Governs adrenaline, stress, the drive to complete, and physical pressure. Biologically associated with the adrenal glands. Approximately 68 percent of people have a defined Root Center. The Root creates pressure that drives action; the pressure is not inherently bad but becomes problematic when the mind uses it as a reason to rush into incorrect decisions.

## Defined vs. Undefined

"Where your definition is your nature, your openness is where you are nurtured." - Ra Uru Hu

**Defined centers**: Always operating. Consistent. The person generates this quality from within. Others entering the aura will be conditioned by it.

**Undefined centers**: Open to conditioning. The person receives and amplifies whatever energy is present in their environment. Over time, the mind builds not-self strategies around maintaining this amplified energy. Undefined centers are also sites of potential wisdom: observing how conditioning flows through them builds understanding that those with the center defined may not have.

## Centers and Types

The configuration of defined centers determines energy type:
- Defined Sacral (without motor-to-throat connection): Generator
- Defined Sacral with motor-to-throat: Manifesting Generator
- Motor to throat without Sacral: Manifestor
- No Sacral, no motor to throat: Projector
- No defined centers: Reflector
