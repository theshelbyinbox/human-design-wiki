# G Center

The G Center (also called the Identity Center or Self Center) is located at the geometric center of the [[Human Design System]] BodyGraph, positioned as a diamond shape. It governs identity, love, and direction in life. The G Center also contains the magnetic monopole -- described as the force that holds the dual crystals of consciousness (personality and design) together within the body and drives the trajectory of a person's life.

## The Magnetic Monopole

The magnetic monopole is unique in Human Design cosmology. Unlike the personality crystal and the design crystal, which are described as dual and separate, the monopole is singular. It is the force that creates the experience of a unified self from two fundamentally different streams of information (conscious and unconscious). The monopole also governs the direction of one's life -- the movement through time and space that constitutes biography.

"Here in the center of the G Center, the identity, in here we have what's called the magnetic monopole and it holds us together. It holds us together in the illusion of our separateness." - Ra Uru Hu

## Identity and Direction

The G Center is the seat of fixed identity and life direction for those with it defined. When the G Center is defined, a person has a consistent, reliable sense of self and direction -- they know who they are and tend to move through life along a coherent trajectory. A defined G Center person is less susceptible to environmental identity shifts.

The G Center also governs love -- not romantic love specifically but the principle of love as direction, as the force that orients a life. Love and direction are treated as closely linked in Human Design: to love what one is moving toward is to be in alignment with the G Center's function.

## Eight Gates of the G Center

The G Center contains eight gates: 1, 2, 7, 10, 13, 15, 25, and 46. These gates are distributed across the wheel in two crosses: the Cross of the Sphinx and the Cross of the Vessel of Love. These crosses are central to the structure of the Incarnation Crosses in the system.

## Undefined G Center

People with an undefined G Center experience identity as fluid and contextual. Who they are shifts depending on who they are with and where they are. This is not a disorder -- it is mechanical. The undefined G Center naturally samples identity from its environment. The challenge arises when this fluidity is interpreted as a problem to be solved (asking "who am I?" or "where am I going?" as persistent anxiety rather than as an invitation to explore).

All [[Reflector]] types have an undefined G Center. For Reflectors, the environment and community are particularly significant because they provide the primary field that the Reflector samples and reflects.

A common not-self question for an undefined G Center is: "Who am I?" or "Where am I going?" Observing these questions as arising from the open center, rather than treating them as crises requiring urgent resolution, is part of deconditioning.

## G Center and Environment

Because an undefined G Center makes a person sensitive to environmental identity, placement becomes important. Undefined G Center people tend to feel most themselves in certain physical spaces and most lost or confused in others. This is not imaginary -- the G Center is genuinely sampling the identity field of the environment. Finding and returning to places that feel right is a practical strategy.

## G Center and [[Self-Projected Authority]]

Some [[Projector]] types with a defined G Center connected to the [[Throat Center]] carry [[Self-Projected Authority]]. For these people, identity (G) expresses through voice (Throat), and clarity about decisions comes through speaking -- hearing oneself say what is true from the G Center's place of love and direction.
