# Inner Authority

Inner Authority in [[Human Design System]] is the body-based decision-making mechanism specific to each person's chart configuration. It represents the answer to the question "how should I decide?" and is determined by which centers are defined in a person's BodyGraph and in what hierarchical order. Inner Authority is paired with [[Strategy]] as the two foundational tools Human Design offers.

The system's central claim regarding authority is that the mind is not an appropriate decision-making organ. The mind is categorized as "outer authority" -- useful for giving advice to others, but not reliable for guiding one's own life, because it is too easily influenced by the conditioning of open centers.

"There is a place within everyone that will give you with absolute clarity your truth in that moment, because it's you. It's not coming out of your openness. It's not coming out of the conditioning of the other." - Ra Uru Hu

## The Hierarchy of Authority

Inner Authority is determined by a hierarchy of centers, from most to least primal. The highest defined center in a person's chart that carries authority becomes their Inner Authority. The order is:

1. [[Solar Plexus Center]] (Emotional Authority) -- defined Solar Plexus takes precedence over all others
2. [[Sacral Center]] (Sacral Authority) -- applies when Solar Plexus is not defined
3. [[Splenic Center]] (Splenic Authority) -- applies when neither Solar Plexus nor Sacral is defined
4. [[Heart Center]] (Ego Authority) -- a specific configuration for some Manifestors and Projectors
5. [[G Center]] (Self-Projected Authority) -- a specific configuration for some Projectors
6. No Inner Authority (Mental Authority / Outer Authority) -- for Mental Projectors who lack inner authority

[[Reflector]] types have no defined centers and use [[Lunar Authority]] -- time over a lunar cycle -- as their decision-making process.

## Emotional Authority

The most common authority, applying to approximately 50 percent of people. Defined by a defined [[Solar Plexus Center]]. The emotional wave -- a constant cycle between high and low feeling states -- means there is never clarity in the present moment. The guideline is "no truth in the now." Decisions should be allowed to settle over time until emotional neutrality or relative clarity is reached. The specific waiting time varies by person and situation, but the principle is consistent: never commit in the heat of an emotional high or the depth of a low.

## Sacral Authority

Applies to Generators and Manifesting Generators who do not have a defined Solar Plexus. The [[Sacral Center]] communicates through immediate, non-verbal sounds: affirmative sounds (uh-huh, mm) or negative sounds (unh-unh, mm-mm). These sounds arise spontaneously in response to yes/no questions or direct life situations and indicate whether the sacral energy is engaged or not. The mind is not involved in producing these sounds; they are bodily responses. Useful practice: ask binary questions of a sacral person and listen for the gut sound rather than the verbal answer.

## Splenic Authority

Applies to people with a defined [[Splenic Center]] but no defined Solar Plexus or Sacral. The spleen communicates through spontaneous intuitive hits -- a quiet, immediate knowing. Splenic authority is in-the-now and does not repeat itself. If the impulse is not acted on, it passes and cannot be recovered. This authority is closely tied to survival instinct, body awareness, and fear. The system's seven fear gates in the spleen (gates 18, 28, 32, 44, 48, 57, 50) are each associated with a specific fear that the spleen uses as a signaling mechanism.

## Ego Authority

A rare authority available to some [[Manifestor]] types and some [[Projector]] types with specific channel configurations connecting the [[Heart Center]] to the Throat or G Center. The Ego Authority expresses through willpower: what the heart truly wants to do and commit to, rather than what the mind thinks should be done. Often manifests through speaking from the heart -- hearing what one says out loud and recognizing whether it carries genuine conviction.

## Self-Projected Authority

Available to some [[Projector]] types with the G Center connected to the Throat but no definition in the emotional, sacral, splenic, or heart centers. Decision-making occurs through speaking -- talking through a decision with trusted others who are simply there to listen. The wisdom emerges in what is said, not in what the listener advises. The person discovers what they know by hearing themselves articulate it.

## Mental Authority (No Inner Authority)

Applies to Mental Projectors -- those with definition only in the Head, Ajna, and/or Throat centers but no definition in the body's centers. These people have no inner authority and must make decisions by getting input from their environment -- talking to multiple people, noticing how different environments feel, attending to what their senses register. The authority is outside, not inside.

## Lunar Authority

Exclusive to [[Reflector]] types. Because Reflectors have no defined centers, they have no consistent inner authority. Instead, they are advised to wait a full lunar cycle before making significant decisions. During this 28-day period, the Moon transits all 64 gates, temporarily creating different definitions in the Reflector's chart. The full cycle gives the Reflector a complete experiential sample before committing.

## Authority and the Mind

Human Design consistently positions the mind's desire to be involved in decisions as the source of most life problems. Open centers generate not-self strategies that the mind uses as its basis for deciding. None of these strategies represent the person's actual authority. The experiment of living by Inner Authority is the experiment of bypassing the mind's agenda and trusting the body's intelligence.
