# Lunar Authority

Lunar Authority is the decision-making framework exclusive to [[Reflector]] types. Because Reflectors have no defined centers, they have no consistent inner authority comparable to sacral sounds, emotional clarity, or splenic intuition. Their authority is time -- specifically the time required for the Moon to complete one full transit of the wheel.

## The Lunar Cycle

The Moon moves through all 64 gates of the wheel approximately every 28 to 29 days. As it transits each gate, it temporarily activates definition in the Reflector's chart. A Reflector will experience different emotional states, different energy levels, different clarity and confusion at different points in the lunar cycle because of these transiting activations.

"A reflector on any given day is only an aspect of who they are. You don't have a whole picture until you go through your cycles." - Ra Uru Hu

Only after experiencing the full range of these temporary activations -- after living with a decision through the entire lunar cycle -- does the Reflector have access to a complete experiential perspective on it. The decision that feels correct across the full cycle is the correct decision.

## Talking Through the Cycle

Reflectors are advised to discuss significant decisions with many different people throughout the lunar waiting period. Not to gather advice, but to sample the field of perspectives. Each conversation reflects something back to the Reflector. The accumulation of these reflections -- alongside the bodily experience of the Moon's transit -- builds the full picture.

## Resistance to Waiting

The greatest challenge for Reflectors with Lunar Authority is external pressure to decide quickly. Modern life runs on rapid response. People around Reflectors may find the 28-day process frustrating or interpret it as indecisiveness. The Human Design position is unequivocal: anyone who cannot give the Reflector the time they need is not correct for them.

"If you are correct in your demand for respect, you will get it. They will wait. And those who do not wait -- you did not lose anything." - Ra Uru Hu

## The Moon as Mapping

Reflectors who map their lunar cycle -- noting which gates the Moon is transiting and how they feel at each point -- develop a personal lunar calendar that becomes a reliable life-planning tool. This mapping makes the lunar cycle tangible and actionable rather than abstract.
