# Minor Authorities

Approximately 96% of the human population carries one of the three major authorities: [[Emotional Authority]] (~50%), [[Sacral Authority]] (~30%), or [[Splenic Authority]] (~15%). The remaining ~4% carries one of the **minor authorities**. These are the configurations Ra Uru Hu grouped together because they share a common precondition: an undefined Solar Plexus, an undefined Sacral, and an undefined Spleen.

> "Today we're going to deal with the minority authorities — a group of authorities that really represent about four out of every hundred people. To have any of these minor authorities, you must have an undefined solar plex center, an open sacral center, and an open splenic center."
> — Ra Uru Hu

Within that constraint, the minor authorities are:

- **Ego-Manifested Authority** (~1%)
- **Ego-Projected Authority** (<0.5%)
- **Self-Projected Authority** (~2.5%)
- **Mental Authority** (~0.5–1%) — covered fully in [[Mental Authority]] and [[No Direct Inner Authority]]
- **Lunar Authority** (~1%) — covered fully in [[Lunar Authority]] and [[No Direct Inner Authority]]

This page focuses on the three minor authorities anchored in the Heart and G centers — Ego-Manifested, Ego-Projected, and Self-Projected — because these are the ones most likely to be misread without careful attention to the configuration.

## Ego-Manifested Authority

Ego-Manifested Authority belongs to a [[Manifestor]] (or rare configurations) whose Heart center is defined and connected to the Throat — either directly through the 45-21 channel or indirectly through the G center.

The precondition: undefined Solar Plexus, undefined Sacral, undefined Spleen.

The Heart center is a motor. When that motor reaches the Throat, decision-making is driven by the will of the Heart and *expressed verbally*. The voice is the instrument.

### Listening to Your Voice

The discipline of Ego-Manifested Authority is to listen to what you actually say in the moment — not to what your mind wants to say, not to what you think you should say, but to what comes out of your mouth.

> "To have ego-manifested authority really means you have to listen to your throat. You have to listen to what you say — what you are saying to the other person — rather than being stuck in what your mind is telling you."
> — Ra Uru Hu

The spontaneous utterance carries the truth. The mind's rehearsed version does not.

> "It is not what you want to say, or what you think you should say, or what you could say — it is precisely what you do say in the moment. At any time. Because what you are expressing is truly your authority when you are not trying to control it."
> — Ra Uru Hu

After-the-fact mental rehearsal — *I should have said this, I could have said that* — is the conditioned mind trying to take back authority. The actual authority is in the unrehearsed moment.

### Surrendering to the Voice

> "Ego-manifested authority is a very, very pure thing. It is about allowing your voice to initiate your life. It is about sitting back and listening. It is about understanding that each and every expression is your perfection. It is only in surrender that your voice says, 'I am going to do this,' and that can be done as a manifestor. It is only when your voice leads that you can follow."
> — Ra Uru Hu

## Ego-Projected Authority

Ego-Projected Authority is rarer — fewer than one in two hundred people. The configuration: the Heart is defined and connected only to the G center (the 21-45 link becomes 21-51, the channel of initiation), with no Heart-to-Throat connection. The Solar Plexus, Sacral, and Splenic centers are all undefined.

Because the Heart is a motor and the G is not, in this configuration the Heart's power dominates the G. This produces a very powerful identity (G center) animated by willpower (Heart), but sitting in a sea of openness across the rest of the chart.

This is a rare form of [[Projector]]. The same projector strategy applies — [[Wait for the Invitation]] — but with intensified stakes:

> "The strategy and authority of the projector become something essential and dominant for these beings: the only thing that they can truly trust is the invitation to initiate. Their life is all about waiting for the invitation to initiate."
> — Ra Uru Hu

### The Bitterness Trap

Ego-Projected Authority beings have no direct voice (no Heart-to-Throat connection), so they cannot express their authority verbally on their own. They must be invited. Without invitation, the powerful internal motor has nowhere to go, and the not-self theme of bitterness intensifies.

> "The ego-projected authority can be a being that is deeply bitter in this life. Bitter that their power to initiate is totally ignored. Bitter because they are exhausted and overwhelmed, trying to be something they are not."
> — Ra Uru Hu

The correct path is mastery and patience: master a system, become recognized, and let the invitation come.

> "Every projector in their life is demanded to master some kind of system. This is the only thing that is really going to make a difference for them. As a projector, when you are prepared, when you can master something, then that invitation will arise."
> — Ra Uru Hu

## Self-Projected Authority

Self-Projected Authority is the G center defined and connected to the Throat, with no definition in the Solar Plexus, Sacral, Spleen, or Heart. The G can have the Ajna above it — Head → Ajna → Throat → G is still Self-Projected — but the key element is the G-to-Throat connection without Heart involvement.

This is approximately 2.5% of the human population. It is exclusive to Projectors who have no defined motors.

### Hearing the Identity Speak

Self-Projected Authority is decision-making through the voice — specifically, the voice of identity. The G center is the seat of identity, love, and direction; the Throat is expression. When the G speaks through the Throat, the person discovers their truth by hearing what they say.

> "The key in any authority connected to the throat: whatever you need to hear, whatever you need to know, you will discover in what you say. And what you say as a projector is according to the invitation."
> — Ra Uru Hu

For a Self-Projected Authority Projector, the practice is:

1. Wait for the invitation (Projector strategy).
2. When invited, talk through the decision aloud — with someone who is simply present to listen.
3. Listen to what comes out of your mouth.
4. The decision is in what you say spontaneously, not what you analyze afterward.

> "When you're invited, listen. Listen to what you say. Hear it. Don't try to figure out what you should say or could say or might say. You are here to trust your spontaneous utterances — what you say in the moment of invitation. That is your truth. You ignore it at your peril."
> — Ra Uru Hu

### The Open Body

The defining feature of Self-Projected Authority is a powerful G-defined identity sitting in an otherwise mostly open chart. No motors, no body-center signals, no emotional wave, no splenic intuition. This makes Self-Projected Projectors particularly susceptible to conditioning across every other center — and their authority depends on having trusted others to talk to.

> "Self-projected authority beings are dealing with a projector that has no motors whatsoever — no root, no sacral, no emotional system, no heart center. They are deeply non-energy beings and, as such, can be totally lost in energy conditioning."
> — Ra Uru Hu

## The Common Theme: Discipline and Courage

All of the minor authorities are easy to misread because they don't operate on the in-the-moment body signals that dominate the major authorities. The voice that carries the truth is fleeting, easy to override mentally, and easy to dismiss as "just talking." The configurations themselves are rare enough that few external models exist for how to live them correctly.

> "All of these minor authorities, like any authority in human design, are a key in your life. Your strategy and authority allows you to navigate on this plane correctly, to move towards the perfection of your being. It does not matter whether your authority is rare or not. What matters is the discipline and the courage to live the correctness of your process."
> — Ra Uru Hu
