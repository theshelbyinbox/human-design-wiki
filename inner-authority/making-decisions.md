# Making Decisions in Practice

Ra Uru Hu said repeatedly that Human Design only ever offered one thing: a way to make decisions correctly as yourself. Every center, every channel, every gate, every transit description in the system is downstream of that one offering. The framework, the chart, the elaborate vocabulary — all of it exists so that a person can identify how their specific body is designed to decide, and then run their life through that mechanism instead of through the mind.

> "I don't get it — it's so simple. I mean, everybody thinks that everything is complex. A lot of people who come to human design think, oh my god, it's so complex. It's not. It's really very simple. It's very simple because it's all based on the same thing: everything is moving. Everything. Everything. Everything is an object moving in space."
> — Ra Uru Hu

> "Human design is only about one thing: it's about offering you the opportunity to understand the best way to make decisions as you, so you can navigate, because that's what this life is about."
> — Ra Uru Hu

## Navigation as the Frame

The mental frame Ra used most consistently was *navigation*. You are an object moving through space, and every decision is a course adjustment. Made correctly, the decision keeps you on a trajectory that is uniquely yours. Made through the mind, the decision sends you off-course onto somebody else's trajectory.

> "If it's moving, then everything about the nature of being, everything about your life and my life, is about navigation — what we call navigation in this life, what we call decision-making. How you make a decision."
> — Ra Uru Hu

> "Making decisions through strategy and authority — it'll get you where you have to go."
> — Ra Uru Hu

## Two Different Time Worlds

One of the most overlooked structural facts about decision-making is that humanity is split nearly in half by timing:

- **About 51%** of humanity has a defined Solar Plexus — emotional beings whose authority operates *over time*.
- **About 49%** of humanity has an undefined Solar Plexus — unemotional beings whose authority operates *in the moment*.

> "Unemotional beings are here fundamentally to operate in the now. Their process is oriented to the existential. They're here to deal with things spontaneously."
> — Ra Uru Hu

> "If you're an emotional being, you're operating in a different time. It's a very different time, yet most emotional human beings don't pay any attention to the fact that they have a different natural timing but try to emulate the timing of those that don't have a defined emotional system."
> — Ra Uru Hu

This means a culture that demands immediate decisions is, by design, asking half the population to operate against their nature. Emotional beings who decide in the moment are not being decisive — they are being not-self. Unemotional beings who agonize over a decision are also being not-self — their design is to know in the moment or to let it pass.

> "You walk up to a person who is unemotional and you say, 'Would you like to?' If they have to agonize over that, if they have to take their time over that, they truly do not know."
> — Ra Uru Hu

## What Waiting Actually Is

The strategies of three of the four types involve some form of waiting. So does the authority of every emotional being, regardless of type. Waiting is the most misunderstood word in Human Design.

> "It's one of those things that so many people when they make human design seem to be confused about. It's a word. It's just a word. It's called waiting. Waiting. So many people think that waiting is a stagnant sort of dead space. You know, nothing is happening. Nothing is going on. Nothing's ever going on. You know, that waiting is a full stop. That waiting is a hesitation. That waiting is something that it's not."
> — Ra Uru Hu

> "Waiting can be translated as a higher state of alertness. In waiting, there are things going on. There is the movement and the process of whatever it is that you're doing. But the waiting is a state. It is a state of awareness. It is being present. It is being ready for precisely those things that you're actually waiting for."
> — Ra Uru Hu

Waiting is not absence. It is presence with a different orientation. The Generator waiting to respond is alert to what is appearing in the environment. The Emotional Authority waiting through the wave is sampling their own internal weather. The Projector waiting for invitation is being visible enough to be seen. The Reflector waiting the lunar cycle is sampling 28 days of different definitions. None of these is doing nothing. All of them are doing the precise opposite of *grabbing*.

## Building the Trust

The hardest part of running the experiment is not the conceptual understanding. It is the trust required to act on a body signal when the mind is screaming a different answer.

The path is incremental. Each decision made through Strategy and Authority that produces a better outcome than the mind would have produced is one piece of evidence. The evidence accumulates. Eventually the mind starts to relax its grip — not because the person decided to trust the body, but because the track record makes the trust mechanical.

This is what Ra called the experiment. There is no leap of faith required. There is only the discipline of running the test, watching the results, and letting the evidence shift the relationship between mind and body over time.

## A Daily Practice

The daily practice of making decisions in Human Design is small and specific:

1. **Notice the decision.** Many decisions get made on autopilot without ever being noticed as decisions. Strategy and Authority require recognizing the moment of choice.
2. **Check the strategy first.** Did this decision arise through your correct type-level engagement (respond, inform, invitation, lunar cycle)? Or are you initiating from the mind?
3. **Check the authority second.** Within the engagement, what does your specific authority say? Sacral sound? Wave-clarity? Splenic hit? Heart-felt conviction? Self-articulation through speech?
4. **Honor the timing.** If the authority is emotional, wait the wave. If splenic, decide now. If sacral, listen for the gut sound. If lunar, take the cycle.
5. **Observe the result.** Did the decision produce the signature state over time? Did it produce the not-self theme? Use the outcome as feedback, not judgment.

## Strategy + Authority as a Pair

Strategy without Authority leaves the decision floating. Authority without Strategy means the body is responding to things it was not meant to respond to in the first place. The two have to be paired.

A Generator with Emotional Authority does not just wait to respond — they also wait through the wave after the sacral lights up. A Projector with Splenic Authority does not just feel a splenic hit — they wait for an invitation to be in the situation where that hit applies. The combinations vary by type and configuration, but the principle is uniform: Strategy says *when and how to engage*, Authority says *how to decide once engaging*.

## Decisions Are the Whole Thing

The recurring claim across Ra's teaching is that there is nothing else.

> "It's about decision-making. This is the whole thing to really grasp about strategy and authority: it's about making a decision. It isn't about anything else."
> — Ra Uru Hu

> "So strategy and authority for the decisions. Let your mind do all the measuring it wants to do. But save the decision-making for yourself, for the essence of what is you, so that you can move along a track that allows you to fulfill your purpose here."
> — Ra Uru Hu
