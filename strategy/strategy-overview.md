# Strategy Overview

In [[Human Design System]], Strategy is the type-specific behavioral guideline that describes how each energy type is designed to engage with life, decisions, and opportunities. Strategy is paired with [[Inner Authority Overview|Inner Authority]] as the two primary practical tools the system offers. The purpose of following one's Strategy is to reduce resistance, minimize the not-self, and over time produce the [[Strategy and Signature|Signature]] state associated with each type.

Strategy does not operate through mental planning or deliberate intention. It is a mechanical description of how each type's aura naturally functions in relation to life and other people.

## The Four Strategies

There is one Strategy per type, with the Manifesting Generator carrying a hybrid of two. Each Strategy addresses a specific challenge in how that type's aura meets the world.

- **[[Respond]]** — for [[Generator]] and [[Manifesting Generator]]; wait for life to present something, then check the sacral response.
- **[[Inform]]** — for [[Manifestor]] (and the second half of the Manifesting Generator strategy); notify those who will be impacted before acting.
- **[[Wait for the Invitation]]** — for [[Projector]]; wait for formal recognition before entering significant life situations.
- **[[Wait the Lunar Cycle]]** — for [[Reflector]]; wait 28 days for the Moon to transit all 64 gates before deciding.

## Strategy Is About Decision-Making

Ra Uru Hu framed the entire Human Design experiment as a navigation problem. We are objects moving in space, and how we move — what we say yes to, what we say no to, what we wait for — defines whether we are living our own life or someone else's.

> "It's about decision-making. This is the whole thing to really grasp about strategy and authority: it's about making a decision. It isn't about anything else."
> — Ra Uru Hu

The mind is not the decision-maker. The mind measures, compares, and runs pro/con lists, but those tools, applied to decisions, route a person through their open centers and into the [[Not-Self]]. Strategy is what bypasses this. It anchors the decision in the body's mechanical response — the sacral sound, the emotional wave, the splenic hit, the lunar cycle — rather than in mental analysis.

## How Strategy Differs from Authority

Strategy and [[Inner Authority Overview|Authority]] work together but address different things.

- **Strategy** is type-level. It tells you *when* and *how* to engage with life.
- **Authority** is configuration-level. It tells you *how to decide* once you are engaging.

For example, a [[Generator]] uses Strategy (respond) to wait for something in the environment to engage with, then uses Authority (sacral sounds, or emotional wave if their Solar Plexus is defined) to determine whether the response is a yes or a no. An emotional Generator who tries to commit in the moment of response is jumping the authority. A Generator who initiates from the mind without waiting for response is jumping the strategy.

## What Strategy Replaces

Strategy is meant to replace the mental decision-making that most people default to from childhood. The conditioning of [[Open Centers|open centers]] generates not-self strategies (need to know everything, need to be loved, need to feel secure, etc.) that the mind then uses as the basis for decisions. None of these strategies represent the person's actual design.

The shift to Strategy is mechanical, not philosophical. It does not require belief. It requires the experiment: apply Strategy to actual decisions and observe what changes.

## The Seven-Year Experiment

[[Deconditioning]] is the long-form outcome of consistent Strategy and Authority practice. Ra placed the timeline at seven years, correlating with the body's cellular renewal cycle. The wiki's [[Deconditioning]] entry covers this in more depth.
