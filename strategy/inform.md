# Inform — Manifestor Strategy

The strategy of Inform applies primarily to [[Manifestor]] types — approximately 8 to 9 percent of the human population — and as the second step of the [[Respond|Respond and Inform]] strategy for [[Manifesting Generator]]s. The Manifestor is the only type that can truly initiate without waiting for an external cue, and Inform is the mechanical adjustment that allows their initiating energy to move through the world with minimum resistance.

## Why Manifestors Need to Inform

The Manifestor aura is closed and repelling. It does not draw others in; it pushes against them. When a Manifestor acts without warning, the impact is felt by everyone in their field — and the typical response from others is suspicion, resistance, or attempts to slow the Manifestor down. None of this is personal. It is the mechanical effect of an unannounced motor moving into space.

Informing addresses this. Informing is not asking permission. It is notification. It tells those who will be affected that something is about to happen, which gives their nervous systems time to adjust and removes the impulse to resist what they didn't see coming.

> "Informing clears the path. It moves things out of the way. It moves mountains, in fact. And it is the only freedom that can be available to the manifestor."
> — Ra Uru Hu

## What to Inform

A Manifestor informs anyone whose life will be affected by an action they are about to take. This includes:

- Partners and family before making decisions that change shared circumstances.
- Coworkers before pivoting on a shared project.
- Friends before changing plans.
- Anyone whose plans depend on the Manifestor's predicted behavior.

What is informed is the *what* — the upcoming action — not the full reasoning or justification. The Manifestor is not opening a discussion. They are extending the courtesy of forewarning so the other person can adjust.

## Children vs Adult Manifestors

Children Manifestors are advised to **ask permission**, not inform. A child living in the home of their parents is operating under their parents' authority and resources. Informing is a strategy for the autonomous adult Manifestor who is responsible for the consequences of their own actions.

This distinction matters in parenting Manifestor children. Manifestor children who are punished or shut down when they try to act without informing or asking learn early to suppress their initiating capacity, and the not-self anger calcifies into something the adult version of them must later unwind.

## The Signature and Not-Self of the Manifestor

When a Manifestor informs consistently, their environment stops fighting them. The closed aura still keeps people at a distance, but the warning removes the surprise — and the Manifestor experiences **peace**, their signature state.

When a Manifestor fails to inform — moves without warning, expects the world to keep up — the not-self theme of **anger** rises. Anger is the friction of an initiating force colliding with the resistance of an environment that wasn't told it was coming.

## Inform Within the Manifesting Generator Strategy

The [[Manifesting Generator]] strategy is **Respond and Inform**. The Manifesting Generator first waits for life to present something, checks the sacral response, and only then — if the response is yes — informs and acts.

When a Manifesting Generator skips informing, the same not-self theme appears: anger, layered onto the Generator's underlying frustration. Manifesting Generators who pivot mid-project without telling their collaborators tend to leave a wake of confusion behind them. Informing slows them down enough to take their environment with them.

## The Practice

The practice of Inform is, mechanically, very small:

- Before doing the thing, say what you are about to do.
- Don't ask permission. Don't justify. Don't soften it into a request.
- Tell the people whose lives or plans will be impacted.
- Then act.

Most resistance to this practice comes from the Manifestor's own conditioning: a sense that informing is weak, redundant, or unnecessary. The experiment is what reveals the truth — informed Manifestors meet far less resistance and arrive at peace far more often than uninformed ones.
