# Right Angle Cross of Consciousness 2

The Right Angle Cross of Consciousness 2 places [[Gate 35]] (Progress) in the Personality Sun and [[Gate 5]] (Waiting) in the Personality Earth. With the Design Sun in [[Gate 63]] and Design Earth in [[Gate 64]], this variant flips the leading edge from doubt to hunger. The carrier is led by the appetite for experience itself — the Throat-level "I've done this, what's next?" — while the Design body holds the mental doubt and confusion in the background as fuel for forward motion. Ra called this cross's lead the gate of progress, the conscious appetite that drives civilization forward. Consciousness 2 people are hungry for what they have not yet experienced.

## Mechanics

- **Personality Sun:** Gate 35 — Progress
- **Personality Earth:** Gate 5 — Waiting / Fixed Rhythms
- **Design Sun:** Gate 63 — Doubt (After Completion)
- **Design Earth:** Gate 64 — Before Completion
- **Quarter:** Quarter of Civilization (Realm of Duhbe, Purpose: Form)
- **Angle:** Right Angle — personal destiny
- **Axis A (Personality):** 5 ↔ 35
- **Axis B (Design):** 63 ↔ 64

## The Four Gates

### Gate 35 (Personality Sun)
[[Gate 35]] is the Throat gate of progress, the hunger for the next experience. As the lead of the Cross of Consciousness 2, Gate 35 names the carrier's orientation: appetite. They want to taste, try, do, see — and the hunger civilizes form because the carrier keeps producing new experiences for the culture to learn from.

### Gate 5 (Personality Earth)
[[Gate 5]] is the Sacral gate of waiting, of fixed rhythms. Beneath the hunger, Gate 5 supplies the rhythmic patience the hunger must learn to honor. Hunger without rhythm becomes consumption; hunger with rhythm becomes ritual.

### Gate 63 (Design Sun)
[[Gate 63]] is the Head doubt, the pressure for logical certainty. In the design body, Gate 63 supplies the underlying inquiry that keeps the hunger from being mindless. The progress is examined; the appetite is questioned.

### Gate 64 (Design Earth)
[[Gate 64]] is the Head confusion, the pressure of the unresolved past. Beneath the surface, Gate 64 keeps the mind humming on the abstract material the hunger keeps producing.

## The Quarter Theme

The Quarter of Civilization is the Quarter of Form. Its purpose is to take mental initiation and turn it into matter. The Cross of Consciousness 2, sitting in this quarter, civilizes consciousness by giving it appetite. Civilization grows in part because of carriers who keep saying "I want to try that" — and Gate 35 is the gate of that wanting. Without it, civilization stagnates; with it, the culture keeps generating new experiences worth examining.

## The Angle Expression

The Right Angle is personal destiny. The Cross of Consciousness 2 person lives the appetite for themselves. They are not here to teach civilization what to want; they are here to want their own experiences and live them. The hunger is intimate to the body.

## Profiles This Cross Manifests In

The Right Angle Cross of Consciousness 2 manifests in the six Right Angle profiles:
[[1/3]] | [[1/4]] | [[2/4]] | [[2/5]] | [[3/5]] | [[3/6]]

The 1/3 wants experience, dives in, collides, and refines the appetite through failure — learning which hungers are theirs and which were borrowed. The 1/4 brings the appetite into the network — the friend who keeps suggesting new things to try. The 2/4 is the natural hungerer, called out by friends to taste what they themselves did not know they would love. The 2/5 is projected onto as the adventurer, expected to bring back stories. The 3/5 collides with experience after experience, learning what progress actually costs, then offers the wisdom outward. The 3/6 spends thirty years gorging on experience, then climbs to the roof to model what was worth wanting.

## Living This Cross

For the Right Angle Cross of Consciousness 2, [[Strategy]] and [[Inner Authority]] keep the hunger pointed at what is theirs to taste. The shadow is the consumer — the carrier whose mind drives Gate 35 into constant new experience because the appetite itself has become an addiction. Nothing is digested. The fixed rhythms of Gate 5 are violated; the body never rests in any one experience long enough to extract its meaning.

The healthy expression is the appetite that has been disciplined by Gate 5's rhythm. The Cross of Consciousness 2 person, in correctness, says yes to new experience when the body responds, and gives the experience enough time to actually become memory. Then the mind underneath (63/64) can examine what happened, and the next hunger arises from that examination rather than from mental restlessness.

Ra noted that Gate 35 is one of the most powerful Throat manifestations — the carrier of this cross often appears outwardly successful, full of stories, well-traveled, well-experienced. The danger is that the inner accumulation is shallow. Without Gate 5's rhythm and 63/64's reflection, the experiences pile up but do not become wisdom. The carrier looks rich but feels empty.

The gift, lived in alignment, is the embodied curriculum. Consciousness 2 people give the culture lived experience to draw from. They are the travelers, the tasters, the experimenters of the Throat. Their hunger is what keeps civilization in motion. The era of progress is partly built on the back of their willingness to keep trying the new thing.

The cross also asks its carriers to grieve the rhythm. Gate 5 demands a slowness the hungry mind hates. Real progress is rhythmic — the seasons of trying, resting, examining, trying again. The carrier who learns this rhythm produces over decades; the carrier who fights it burns out in years.

For Cross of Consciousness 2 people specifically, the work of integrating Gate 5 underneath is the central task of midlife. The early years are usually a sprint of experience-collection. Somewhere in midlife the body says enough, and the carrier either learns rhythm or breaks. The ones who learn rhythm become the most interesting elders of their generation — full of stories, full of integrated wisdom, finally able to sit still long enough for what they encountered to mean something. The ones who do not learn rhythm become exhausted, often physically ill, with cabinets full of unfinished projects and notebooks full of trips they cannot remember the details of. The choice is real, and Strategy and Authority is how the choice gets made body-first instead of mind-first.
