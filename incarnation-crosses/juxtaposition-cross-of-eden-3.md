# Juxtaposition Cross of Conflict

The Juxtaposition Cross of Conflict is the 4/1 fixed-fate variant that sits between the Right Angle Cross of Eden 3 and the Left Angle Cross of the Plane 2 in the Quarter of Duality. The Personality Sun remains in Gate 6 — Conflict — but the Design axis rotates to the Vessel of Love axis 10/15, replacing the individual articulation channel with the love-of-self and love-of-humanity axis.

Ra noted that this is where the underlying theme of love emerges for the first time in the second half of the Quarter of Duality. The individual articulation channel of 12/11 that defined Eden 3 is gone; in its place is the 10/15 axis that carries behavior and rhythm. The carrier is fixed in the conflict of bonded life, but with the underlying potential to love themselves and to love humanity through their behavior and rhythm.

## Mechanics

- **Personality Sun:** Gate 6 (Conflict / Friction)
- **Personality Earth:** Gate 36 (Crisis / Darkening of the Light)
- **Design Sun:** Gate 10 (Treading / Behavior of the Self)
- **Design Earth:** Gate 15 (Modesty / Rhythm)
- **Quarter:** Quarter of Duality (Realm of Jupiter, Purpose: Bonding)
- **Angle:** Juxtaposition — Fixed Fate
- **Profile:** 4/1 only

The Personality axis is the Solar Plexus emotional bonding axis 6/36 — friction and crisis. The Design axis is the Vessel of Love axis 10/15 — behavior and rhythm. The conscious mind is fixed in the experience of bonded conflict; the unconscious carries the potential for self-love and universal love.

## The Four Gates

### Gate 6 (Personality Sun) — Conflict / Friction

[[Gate 6]] leads the cross. The Conflict being is identified with friction in bonded life. The Juxtaposition fixedness locks the carrier into a single archetypal conflict pattern that defines their relational life.

### Gate 36 (Personality Earth) — Crisis / Darkening of the Light

[[Gate 36]] grounds the conscious mind in crisis. The conflict produces the crisis, and the crisis is the carrier's ongoing context.

### Gate 10 (Design Sun) — Treading / Behavior of the Self

[[Gate 10]] is the unconscious G Center gate of self-behavior. The Conflict being carries extreme variations of behavior at the level of design. The 4/1 profile combined with this gate produces a carrier whose behavioral patterns are locked and unusual.

### Gate 15 (Design Earth) — Modesty / Rhythm

[[Gate 15]] grounds the unconscious in extreme rhythms. The Conflict being moves through extremes of activity, withdrawal, intensity, and stillness — the rhythms of their fixed fate.

## The Quarter Theme

The Quarter of Duality places this Juxtaposition in the realm where bonding is the purpose. The Conflict being lives the bonding question as a fixed pattern of friction. Ra noted that the cross does not like people — there is a structural difficulty with how others behave, with their frequency, with their patterns — and this difficulty becomes the organizing principle of the carrier's relational life.

## The Angle Expression

Juxtaposition crosses carry Fixed Fate. The Conflict being is locked in the conflict pattern. The carrier cannot easily soften the friction, cannot easily widen their tolerance, cannot easily transform the conflict into peace.

## Profiles This Cross Manifests In

Juxtaposition Crosses appear exclusively in the 4/1 profile.

## Living This Cross

For the 4/1 Conflict being, the work is to inhabit the fixed conflict with as much awareness as possible. The cross is structurally oriented toward demanding control over the relational environment — the carrier will only bond with those who can behave according to their standards, and the standards are non-negotiable.

Ra noted: "I am only going to have a child with you if I can teach you how to behave. I am only going to have a relationship with you if I can get you to function in a way that is acceptable to me." This is the structural reality of the Conflict Juxtaposition. The carrier is not flexible about who they bond with; the bonding is conditional on the partner accepting the carrier's behavioral and rhythmic standards.

The shadow path is the carrier who confuses this structural reality with personal righteousness, who concludes that they are simply correct about how everyone should behave and that everyone else is failing. The cross can produce the controlling, dominating partner who exhausts their bonds through unrelenting demands for behavioral conformity.

The medicine is awareness combined with surrender to the structural fixedness. The Conflict being cannot stop being conflictual; the work is to be conflictual with as much consciousness and care as possible. The 10 self-love at the unconscious level must be honored; without self-love, the conflict becomes purely punitive. The 15 universal rhythm must be honored; without it, the conflict becomes purely personal.

The 4 profile means the carrier's relational life operates within a defined network. The Conflict being's friction is engaged within a close circle — the few partners, friends, and family members who can tolerate the structural conflict and who benefit from it in their own development. The 1 unconscious means the carrier requires deep foundational study; on this cross, the study is often of behavior itself, of relational patterns, of what makes bonded life work.

When the cross is lived consciously, the Conflict being becomes a fixed point of standards in their close circle. They are the friend or partner who will not accept sloppy behavior, who demands clarity, who refuses to soften their requirements out of social pressure. This can be exhausting for the close circle and also clarifying — the Conflict being teaches by their unyielding standards what care and precision in bonded life actually require.

The cross also has the underlying possibility of mutating into love when the unconscious 10/15 surfaces. Ra noted that this is where love begins to emerge in the second half of the Quarter of Duality. The Conflict being who recognizes the love operating beneath their conflict — the love of the self that demands their standards be honored, the love of humanity that requires others to be their full selves — can soften the conflict from punitive to relational. This is rare but possible, and when it occurs, the Conflict cross delivers something that no other cross can deliver: standards held with love rather than with grievance.
