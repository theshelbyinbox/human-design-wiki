# Left Angle Cross of Service 2

The Left Angle Cross of Service 2 is named in Ra Uru Hu's teaching as the Left Angle Cross of Demands 1. The four Service gates — 52, 58, 17, 18 — rotate into a Left Angle configuration in the Quarter of Civilization, producing a transpersonal life whose karmic work is the placement of demands on others. The Demands 1 carrier is here to require something of the people around them. Their stillness is not passive. It is the still pressure that asks the surrounding system to rise to a standard. Where the Right Angle Service 2 carrier concentrates on their own work, the Left Angle Demands 1 carrier concentrates the people around them — placing on them the steady pressure that produces, over time, the development those people were capable of all along.

## Mechanics

- **Personality Sun:** Gate 52 — Keeping Still
- **Personality Earth:** Gate 58 — Vitality
- **Design Sun:** Gate 17 — Opinion
- **Design Earth:** Gate 18 — Correction
- **Quarter:** Quarter of Civilization (Realm of Duhbe, Purpose of Form)
- **Angle:** Left Angle — Transpersonal Destiny

Same four gates as the Right Angle Service 2 but lived through a transpersonal angle. The carrier's life will not make sense as a personal story. It makes sense as the people whose work improved because the carrier was still enough to demand it.

## The Four Gates

### Gate 52 (Personality Sun) — Keeping Still

The conscious work of concentrated demand. For the Demands 1 carrier, the 52 is not just personal concentration; it is the still pressure that focuses the people around them. The carrier sits in a way that demands the room's attention. The stillness is the medium of the demand.

### Gate 58 (Personality Earth) — Vitality

Adrenal joy as the conscious ground. The 58 keeps the demand alive rather than oppressive. The carrier loves life enough to require that the people around them participate fully in it. Without the 58, the demand becomes a burden. With it, the demand becomes an invitation to rise.

### Gate 17 (Design Sun) — Opinion

Mental opinion beneath the conscious work. The 17 supplies the carrier with the mental clarity about what should be demanded. The opinion is unconscious — running beneath the stillness — but it shapes what the carrier holds people accountable for.

### Gate 18 (Design Earth) — Correction

Splenic correction as the underlying medium. The 18 ensures that the demands are grounded in body-wisdom about what is actually broken. The carrier does not demand arbitrary standards. The demands track what the spleen knows is rotten and needs refining.

## The Quarter Theme

The Quarter of Civilization is the realm of form. Its purpose is to build the material world. The Demands 1 carrier contributes to that civilising work by holding others to standards they would otherwise let slide. The carrier is the still pressure at the centre of the build — the one whose presence ensures that the work gets done correctly.

Ra emphasised that the Left Angle Cross of Demands is a difficult cross to live well. The carrier becomes a thorn in many people's sides. The demands are real. The standards are high. The pressure is sustained. The carrier is not popular in the short term. They are necessary in the long term.

## The Angle Expression

A Left Angle Cross is a cross of transpersonal destiny. The life is not the carrier's own. The work is publicising — making available to the collective what the Right Angles have lived through. The Demands 1 publicises the standards of sustained concentration: the carrier walks among the half-finished works and lets the still pressure of their presence raise the standard.

The not-self distortion of this cross is the carrier who demands from mind, without invitation, becoming a critical and exhausting presence in the surrounding system. The mind decides what should be demanded and pushes the demand onto everyone, and the result is the carrier known as impossible to please. The correct expression is body-led demand. The body's stillness is the medium. The carrier sits, and the people around them feel the demand without needing it spoken.

Ra called Left Angles karmic lives. The Demands 1 carrier will meet, again and again, the same patterns of half-effort and unfinished work, and the work is to hold the still pressure until those patterns shift. The carrier rarely sees the full shift. The work continues across years.

## Profiles This Cross Manifests In

The Left Angle of this cross is carried by five profiles: 4/6, 5/1, 5/2, 6/2 and 6/3.

- **4/6** is the externalising friend whose demands reach the collective through the friendship network — friends rise to the carrier's standards
- **5/1** is the heretic with foundation, the universalising demand-bringer whose standards have the authority of 1st line study behind them
- **5/2** is the heretic hermit, projected upon as a savior whose demands are seen as universal — most effective when allowed to remain selective
- **6/2** is the role model emerging from the rooftop — the elder whose still demands shape the community
- **6/3** is the role model who moved through trial and error and now demands from a place of demonstrated experience

## Living This Cross

[[Strategy]] and [[Inner Authority]] are non-negotiable. A Left Angle carrier who lives by mind quickly becomes intolerable to the people around them — the chronic demander whose standards exhaust the field. The work is to let the body sit, let the stillness be the medium, and let the demands arrive through presence rather than language.

The signature of this cross lived well is the slow rise of the people around the carrier — the colleague who learned to finish their work, the partner who learned to show up fully, the child who learned the meaning of sustained effort. The carrier rarely takes credit. The work is invisible from the inside.

The not-self signature is the carrier who has become a nag, an critic, an impossible boss — and who exhausts both themselves and everyone in their orbit. The recovery is to come back to the body, sit, and let the still pressure replace the spoken demand.

When the carrier honours this cross, they become what Ra implied was one of the necessary high-standard-holders of the wheel — the one whose presence requires the surrounding system to rise. This is a karmic role. The carrier did not choose it. The work is to host it gracefully.

This is the cross of the still demander, the high-standard concentrator, the body whose presence is itself a request. Lived correctly, it is a service that raises the people around the carrier. Lived incorrectly, it is a life of friction and resentment. The difference is whether the carrier remains in the body.
