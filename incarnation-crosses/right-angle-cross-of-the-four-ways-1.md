# Right Angle Cross of the Four Ways 1

The Right Angle Cross of the Four Ways 1 is the closing cross of the Quarter of Initiation. The Personality Sun sits in Gate 24, the gate of Rationalizing and Returning, and the carrier lives a life shaped around the four nodal phases that turn the human being through the wheel. Where every other cross is named for a quality or theme, this cross is named after the four directions themselves. The four gates of this cross are the only four gates in the I Ching whose names describe a way — Approach, Retreat, Coming to Meet, and Return.

Ra Uru Hu loved this cross. He called it deeply connected to the Nodes and described its carriers as people who go through four 18.6-year cycles of radical thematic change. "The Cross of the Four Ways beings provide the direction for the alignment of others, but have no sense of alignment themselves. For them it seems like there's always, somewhere down the road, a sharp right or a left."

## Mechanics

- **Personality Sun:** Gate 24 — Rationalizing / Return
- **Personality Earth:** Gate 44 — Coming to Meet
- **Design Sun:** Gate 19 — Approach
- **Design Earth:** Gate 33 — Retreat
- **Quarter:** Quarter of Initiation (Realm of Alcyone, Purpose of Mind)
- **Angle:** Right Angle — Personal Destiny

The Personality axis 24–44 is the consciousness: the mind that rationalises by returning to the same thought endlessly, partnered with the splenic gate that meets what arrives in the present. The Design axis 19–33 is the body's vehicle: the tribal need that approaches the other and the cognitive gate that retreats to process what was met.

## The Four Gates

### Gate 24 (Personality Sun) — Rationalizing

The conscious purpose. Gate 24 is the individual mental gate of return. It is the part of the Ajna that processes the same question by going over it and over it until satori — until the inexplicable click of insight arrives. Ra called the 24 the great gate of Rationalizing. As the lead of this cross it gives the carrier a mind that is built to return to what has not yet been understood, again and again, until the understanding arrives.

### Gate 44 (Personality Earth) — Coming to Meet

The ground beneath the rationalising mind. Gate 44 is the splenic gate of pattern-recognition through smell — the tribal awareness that recognises what is being met by remembering what was met before. As the Earth of this cross it grounds the rationalising mind in a body that knows by smell whether a present encounter is a re-encounter with a familiar pattern. The 44 keeps the 24 honest about what the return is actually returning to.

### Gate 19 (Design Sun) — Approach

The unconscious tribal engine. Gate 19 is the Root pressure of approach — the tribal need to draw close to resources, shelter, and the body of the other. Beneath the conscious mind of return, the body of this carrier is built to move toward what it needs. The 19 is the unconscious approach beneath the conscious return.

### Gate 33 (Design Earth) — Retreat

The unconscious cognitive ground. Gate 33 is the Throat gate of retreat — the privacy required for processing what was met. The body of this carrier knows when to step back from the encounter to digest it. The 33 is the unconscious retreat beneath the conscious return — the silent withdrawal during which the next round of rationalising happens.

## The Quarter Theme

The Quarter of Initiation is the realm of mind. Gate 24 closes this quarter at the level of mental processing. The Four Ways 1 carrier contributes to the mental quarter by being the body in which mind goes over the same question until it cracks. The mind initiated through this cross is one that learns by returning — by refusing to abandon the question until the return delivers the insight.

## The Angle Expression

A Right Angle Cross is a personal destiny. The Four Ways 1 carrier lives their own life in four 18.6-year nodal cycles, each with its own radical theme. Ra described this with affection: an old friend of his lived a very poor early life, married a doctor at 19, became part of a straight middle-class world, then at 37 walked away and ended up living on Ibiza as a freak. Four ways. Each cycle complete in itself, each disconnected from the one before.

The not-self distortion is the carrier who fights the cycle — who tries to be one thing across the whole life, who refuses the next theme when it arrives, or who treats the change as failure. The correct expression lets the nodal turn happen, lets the next way reveal itself when the previous way is exhausted, and trusts that the four ways are the structure of the life.

## Profiles This Cross Manifests In

The Right Angle is carried by the six classical right-angle profiles: 1/3, 1/4, 2/4, 2/5, 3/5, and 3/6. Ra noted that the 1/3 on this cross can do very well financially because "they are trial-and-error people, and they're going to be on a roller coaster of a life… by going through several ways in their process as 3s and finding out what doesn't work, they can turn that into an experiential base that gives them opportunities on the material plane."

- **1/3** the investigator who gains enormous experience across the four ways and turns it into material opportunity
- **1/4** the investigator whose returns happen inside a stable friendship network
- **2/4** the natural hermit whose four ways are processed through known relationships
- **2/5** the called hermit projected onto as a saviour at each turn of the cycle
- **3/5** the trial-and-error mutator whose mistakes accumulate into practical wisdom over the four phases
- **3/6** the role-model experimenter who moves through the three life phases inside the four 18.6-year cycles

## Living This Cross

[[Strategy]] and [[Inner Authority]] are the only reliable navigation system. The Four Ways 1 carrier is particularly tempted to mental override because the 24 produces such convincing rationalisations. The work is to let the body, not the mind, signal when the next way has arrived. A Generator with this cross responds with the sacral to the new direction; a Projector waits for the invitation; a Manifestor informs before initiating the next phase; a Reflector lets a full lunar cycle reveal whether the new way is correct.

The signature of the cross lived well is a life that arrives, at its end, having lived four lives. The not-self signature is the carrier who refused the turn and stayed in the first way long after it had stopped feeding them. The work is to trust the nodal mechanic and let the rationalising mind do its job — return to the question until the next way reveals itself.

When the carrier honours the design, they become what Ra described as the alignment-giver for others: people around them feel newly directed by the carrier's life, even as the carrier themselves rarely feels aligned. The four ways are the gift the carrier gives to a world that needs to see direction modelled in real time.
