# Juxtaposition Cross of the Sphinx 2

The Juxtaposition Cross of the Sphinx 2 carries Gates 2, 1, 13 and 7 with the Personality Sun in Gate 2, the Receptive — at the angle of fixed fate. The carrier of this cross is a 4/1 profile, the Opportunist-Investigator, with the directional gift of Gate 2 as the fixed, non-negotiable centre of their life.

Where the right-angle Sphinx 2 directs their own form and the left-angle Sphinx 2 directs through karmic broadcast, the Juxtaposition Sphinx 2 directs from a fixed position in their close-relational field. The carrier's sense of where the form must go is precise, narrow, and applied to the specific people they are externally fixed with.

## Mechanics

- **Personality Sun:** Gate 2 — The Receptive (Higher Knowing / The Driver)
- **Personality Earth:** Gate 1 — The Creative
- **Design Sun:** Gate 13 — The Listener
- **Design Earth:** Gate 7 — Role of the Self
- **Quarter:** Quarter of Civilization (Realm of Duhbe, Purpose of Form)
- **Angle:** Juxtaposition — Fixed Fate
- **Profile:** 4/1 only

The four gates are identical to the right-angle and left-angle Sphinx 2. The mechanical distinction of the Juxtaposition is the 4/1 profile carrying the cross, which produces a life of fixed direction expressed through close-network bonds.

## The Four Gates

### Gate 2 (Personality Sun) — The Receptive / Higher Knowing

The conscious purpose is the body's pre-cognitive sense of direction. In the Juxtaposition the direction is fixed. The carrier knows, with quiet certainty, where their life is supposed to go — and that knowledge is precise rather than expansive. There is one direction; the body holds it; the work is to follow it.

### Gate 1 (Personality Earth) — The Creative

The grounding originator. Gives the directional sense a creative signature that is unmistakably the carrier's own. In the Juxtaposition, this signature is also fixed — one voice, one aesthetic, one particular way of expressing the directional knowing.

### Gate 13 (Design Sun) — The Listener / Fellowship of Man

The unconscious witness. The body absorbs the stories of the close-network and uses what it has heard to refine the direction it broadcasts.

### Gate 7 (Design Earth) — The Army / Role of the Self

The unconscious carrier of role. Beneath the directional work there is an underlying identity that the close network recognises as the alpha — quiet, fixed, the natural reference point of the group.

## The Quarter Theme

The Quarter of Civilization is the quarter of form. Sphinx 2 opens it with the directional engine. The Juxtaposition variant of that engine is fixed and precise — the carrier directs one specific small civilization, namely the network of bonds they are already meaningfully inside.

The cross is not designed for grand-scale civilization-building. It is designed for the precise direction of a specific field — a family, a household, a workshop, a community of close practitioners. The fixed fate of this cross is that the direction is delivered through that intimate scale, again and again, in the same kind of way.

## The Angle Expression

The Juxtaposition is fixed fate. The carrier has one role, one direction, one configuration. The mind's protest against the narrowness is the most common not-self signature — the carrier who wants to be bigger, broader, more publicly directional, and who refuses to honour the precise mechanical placement of their actual life.

The correct expression of this cross is the carrier who recognises the precision of their design and lives into it. The 4/1 profile demands a foundation; the carrier needs the close network and the studied investigation to anchor the directional gift. Without those anchors, the directional impulse becomes restless and ineffective.

For Sphinx 2 in the Juxtaposition, this means the carrier's body knows where to go for one specific group of people, and that knowing is the carrier's contribution. They are not a public director. They are a fixed point of orientation for the network they are already inside.

## Profiles This Cross Manifests In

The Juxtaposition Cross manifests in **4/1 only** — the Opportunist-Investigator. The 4th line carries the close network through which the work lands; the 1st line carries the foundation of investigation that must be done before the direction can be trusted.

For Sphinx 2 in this profile, the investigation work is into direction itself — the carrier studies how their own body knows where to go, refines the pattern, and then lets the direction flow into the close network. The directional gift is given to the group through the 4th line's bonds, not through public broadcast.

The 4/1 is the only profile compatible with the Juxtaposition angle. Where the right-angle profiles carry the cross for the personal-destiny tasks of the wheel, and the left-angle profiles carry it for the transpersonal-karma tasks, the 4/1 carries the Juxtaposition for the tasks of fixed-fate precision — narrow roles delivered through close-network bonds.

## Living This Cross

[[Strategy]] and [[Inner Authority]] govern correct movement. The directional gift is precise; the work of the carrier is to trust it and to let it land in the close field without trying to amplify it for a larger audience.

The not-self signature is the Juxtaposition Sphinx 2 carrier exhausted by the gap between the precision of their actual life and the imagined breadth of the life their mind has invented. The mind says the direction should be public, transformative, civilizational; the body says the direction is for the household, the family, the small group. The carrier in protest never settles. The carrier in surrender produces, over decades, a small but unmistakably-correct civilization inside the network they live with.

Ra Uru Hu's note about the receptive applies in heightened form here: the form knows where it has to go. For the Juxtaposition Sphinx 2 carrier, where the form has to go is a small, specific, fated set of places — and the work of the life is to go to those places and not somewhere else.
