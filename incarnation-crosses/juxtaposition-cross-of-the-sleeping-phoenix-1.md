# Juxtaposition Cross of the Sleeping Phoenix 1

The Juxtaposition Cross of the Sleeping Phoenix 1 is the fixed-fate variant of the Sleeping Phoenix gate set in the Quarter of Initiation. Ra Uru Hu named this configuration the Juxtaposition Cross of Moods. The name points directly at the carrier's reality: a 4/1 profile whose entire life is fixed around the moodiness of the 55th gate, locked into a specific relationship with the spirit-pulse that runs through them.

The one-line theme: the embodied mood-pulse of mutating spirit. The carrier is moodiness made personality, a fixed fate around the emotional uncertainty that the Sleeping Phoenix carries into the world.

## Mechanics

- **Personality Sun:** Gate 55 — Spirit / Abundance
- **Personality Earth:** Gate 59 — Dispersion
- **Design Sun:** Gate 20 — Contemplation
- **Design Earth:** Gate 34 — The Power of the Great
- **Quarter:** Quarter of Initiation
- **Angle:** Juxtaposition — Fixed Fate

The Juxtaposition shares the gate set of the Right Angle Cross of the Sleeping Phoenix 1 but manifests exclusively in the 4/1 profile. The personality is fixed around the moods of the 55, and the design is fixed around the busyness of the 20/34 connection when it appears in the chart. The carrier cannot escape either the moodiness or the activity; they are the two fixed faces of the fate.

Ra pointed out that this is where the gate set's mood quality concentrates most intensely. When the 20/34 connection is missing in the chart (as can happen depending on which of the two design gates the carrier picks up), the carrier loses the busyness that would otherwise distract from the mood. They cannot escape the 55. The not-knowing becomes the entirety of the conscious experience.

## The Four Gates

### Gate 55 (Personality Sun) — Spirit / Abundance

In the Juxtaposition fixed-fate position, the carrier's consciousness is locked into the moodiness of the 55. They live in the wave; they are the wave. Their entire personality is shaped by the question of whether spirit is present right now, and they cannot put the question down.

### Gate 59 (Personality Earth) — Dispersion

The 59 grounds the moodiness in the body's intimate biology. The carrier's social and sexual life is also fixed — they relate to the other in one specific moody pattern, repeated across their lifetime.

### Gate 20 (Design Sun) — Contemplation

The body's design is the now. In the 4/1 fixed harmony, the carrier's vehicle is anchored in the present, but the consciousness rarely arrives there. The body is in the now while the mind is in the mood.

### Gate 34 (Design Earth) — The Power of the Great

The fuel — when present in the design. Ra noted that the busyness of the 34 can sometimes save the Juxtaposition Moods carrier from the depth of the 55's uncertainty by giving them something to do. When the 34 connects to the 20 in the chart, the carrier becomes a moody Manifesting Generator who burns the uncertainty off in activity. When it does not connect, the moodiness dominates.

## The Quarter Theme

The Quarter of Initiation is the realm of mind. Within this quarter, the Juxtaposition Moods 1 serves the specific function of embodying the mood itself — being the fixed reminder that human emotionality is in mutation. The Right Angle Sleeping Phoenix lives the mutation as personal destiny. The Left Angle Spirit broadcasts the mutation as transpersonal art. The Juxtaposition Moods *is* the mood, fixed in a body, walking through the world as a constant signal of the chemistry that is changing.

Ra spoke of this configuration as deeply creative when it touches art — moodiness is the muse, and the 55 is the gate where the muse lives most densely. Many artists with this Juxtaposition carry their entire creative output as the public form of their private wave. Others, less able to channel the wave, become chronically depressive or socially withdrawn.

## The Angle Expression

A Juxtaposition Cross is fixed fate. The 4/1 carrier cannot be argued out of their moodiness, cannot be reasoned into stability, cannot be persuaded that the wave is not as terrible as it feels. They are what they are. To live with a Juxtaposition Moods carrier is to accept the wave as part of the climate.

For Juxtaposition Moods 1, the fixed fate is to be the visible mood-pulse of the Sleeping Phoenix theme. The carrier's relationships, work, and creative output all bear the imprint of the wave. Their friends learn to anticipate it; their partners must be the kind of being who can hold space for the fluctuation without trying to fix it.

The not-self distortion is the carrier who hates their moodiness and tries to numb it with food, sex, substance, or busyness. The same gate (55) Ra called the place of the deepest food problems and the most severe sexual compensations. The correct expression is the carrier who treats the wave as the climate — moodiness as the weather of their life — and lets the wave do what it does without judgement.

## Profiles This Cross Manifests In

Juxtaposition crosses manifest in the 4/1 profile only. This profile is what Ra called the "hermaphrodite" — not Right Angle, not Left Angle, something else. Approximately 1% of the population carries any given Juxtaposition cross.

## Living This Cross

To live this cross correctly is to follow [[Strategy]] and [[Inner Authority]] and to accept that the moodiness is not a problem to solve. The 4/1 carrier has a specific [[Inner Authority]] in their design (often emotional, given that the 55 is itself emotional) and the authority is the way out of the trap of the mind trying to manage the wave.

A carrier living from [[Not-Self]] spends a lifetime fighting their own emotional weather. They diagnose themselves with mood disorders, attempt every cure, never recognise that the wave is the design and not a malfunction. Living from [[Signature]] means accepting the fluctuation and using it — particularly creatively, since the 55 + 59 + 34 combination is one of the most creatively gifted on the wheel.

The deepest practice for this cross is letting the muse be moody. The carrier does not get to produce on a schedule. They produce when the wave allows, and they rest when the wave does not. Their output is rare but irreplaceable — a fixed-fate signal of what creative spirit looks like when it is allowed to fluctuate without being forced.
