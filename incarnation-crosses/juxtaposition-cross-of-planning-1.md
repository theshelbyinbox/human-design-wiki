# Juxtaposition Cross of Bargains

The Juxtaposition Cross of Bargains is the fixed 4/1 variant of the Cross of Planning anchored in the Quarter of Initiation. With the Personality Sun in [[Gate 37]] and the Personality Earth in [[Gate 40]], it shares the leading axis of the Right Angle Cross of Planning 1, but the Juxtaposition gives it a unique flavor: the 4/1 fate is fixed, the carrier is locked into a single thematic, and the bargain is non-negotiable. Ra named this cross "Bargains" because the carrier is the bargain. They do not adjust to other people's terms. The bargain they bring is the bargain they live, and it is the only one available.

## Mechanics

- **Personality Sun:** Gate 37 — The Family / Friendship
- **Personality Earth:** Gate 40 — Deliverance
- **Design Sun:** Gate 16 — Enthusiasm
- **Design Earth:** Gate 9 — Focus (The Taming Power of the Small)
- **Quarter:** Quarter of Initiation (Realm of Alcyone, Purpose: Mind)
- **Angle:** Juxtaposition — fixed fate, 4/1 only
- **Axis A (Personality):** 37 ↔ 40
- **Axis B (Design):** 9 ↔ 16

## The Four Gates

### Gate 37 (Personality Sun)
[[Gate 37]] is the mouth of the bargain — the gate of friendship, touch, and tribal agreement. In the Juxtaposition Cross of Bargains, Gate 37 is everything. The carrier is the bargain made flesh. Whatever they bring to a relationship is the terms, full stop.

### Gate 40 (Personality Earth)
[[Gate 40]] is the lone will of deliverance. Beneath the bargain, Gate 40 supplies the unmoving stomach that says "these are my terms, take them or don't." The fixedness of the Juxtaposition gives Gate 40 unusual stubbornness — the deliverer does not adjust.

### Gate 16 (Design Sun)
[[Gate 16]] is the Throat enthusiasm. In the design body, Gate 16 supplies the experimental surface — the artistic willingness — that makes the fixed bargain palatable enough for others to engage with.

### Gate 9 (Design Earth)
[[Gate 9]] is the Sacral focus. Beneath the surface, Gate 9 supplies the concentration the carrier needs to actually live out the bargain over time.

## The Quarter Theme

The Quarter of Initiation is the Quarter of Mind. The Juxtaposition Cross of Bargains, sitting in this quarter, frames the bargain as a mental initiation — the carrier initiates relationships through the terms they bring. Ra described 4/1 Juxtaposition carriers as exceptionally fixed because the 4 is externalizing (always in relationship) and the 1 is the foundation (must know the bottom of what it stands on). Combined with the 37/40, this means the carrier of Bargains externalizes a fixed agreement to everyone they meet, and that agreement is foundational — the 1st line gives it the ground from which it cannot move.

## The Angle Expression

Juxtaposition crosses are fixed fates. The 4/1 profile is the only profile in which Juxtaposition manifests. The carrier is not personally karmic (Right Angle) and not transpersonally karmic (Left Angle); they are juxtaposed against a fixed theme that does not negotiate. The bargain is the bargain. The carrier cannot dilute it.

Ra was direct about this cross: "Any time you're dealing with a 4/1 bargain, it's their bargain; that's all. It's their bargain. If you don't want to go along with their bargain and their terms, nothing works." The fixedness is not stubbornness as a personality flaw. It is the design. The carrier was built to hold the bargain steady.

## Profiles This Cross Manifests In

[[4/1]] — the only profile in which the Juxtaposition manifests.

The 4/1 carries the externalizing friendliness of the 4th line together with the investigative foundation of the 1st line. They make friends easily, they cannot adjust their terms easily, and the combination gives the Juxtaposition Cross of Bargains a particular signature: the warm, social, communally-embedded person who is also, beneath the warmth, completely fixed on what they will agree to.

## Living This Cross

For the Juxtaposition Cross of Bargains, [[Strategy]] and [[Inner Authority]] are crucial. Because the cross is fixed, the carrier cannot decondition out of it — they will live the bargain whether or not they understand it. What they can do is learn to live it honestly. The shadow is the carrier who pushes their bargain on people who never agreed to it, demanding loyalty to terms they never signed. The healthy expression is the carrier who lets the bargain speak for itself, lets others either accept or decline, and stops trying to convert anyone who walked away.

The Bargains carrier is often someone whose friendships are intense, whose family loyalties are unshakable, and whose grievances last forever. They will keep voting for Joe, in Ra's example, decades after Joe turned out to be a jerk — because they made their bargain with Joe, and the bargain does not adjust just because the facts changed. This is a feature, not a bug. Some bargains need to be kept that long. The Bargains carrier is one of the few designs that can keep them.

The gift, lived in alignment, is loyalty. The carrier of the Juxtaposition Cross of Bargains is the friend who shows up at funerals decades after the friendship was active. They are the family member who remembers everyone's birthday. They are the one whose bargain with a community holds even when the community forgot why the bargain existed. This kind of fixed loyalty is rare and valuable; it is the social glue that keeps tribal bonds alive across generations.

The danger is when the bargain becomes the trap. A bargain made unconsciously in childhood, never renegotiated as the body grew up, can imprison the carrier for life. Strategy and Authority are the only honest way to know which bargains belong to you and which were inherited from a not-self mind. The 4/1 cannot escape the fixedness, but they can choose, with their body, which bargain to give the fixedness to.
