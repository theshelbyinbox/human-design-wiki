# Right Angle Cross of the Sphinx 2

The Right Angle Cross of the Sphinx 2 sits at the very opening of the Quarter of Civilization. Its leading gate — the Personality Sun in Gate 2, the Receptive — is the first gate of the entire second quarter of the wheel. Carriers of this cross are mechanically built to give the form of civilization its direction. They are not the storytellers of the past; they are the drivers of where the form must go next.

This is the cross of the magnetic monopole's directional engine. Beneath every great social structure — every settled tribe, every walled city, every cultivated field — somebody had to know which way to point the people. Sphinx 2 is the cross of that pointing.

## Mechanics

- **Personality Sun:** Gate 2 — The Receptive (Higher Knowing / The Driver)
- **Personality Earth:** Gate 1 — The Creative (Self-Expression)
- **Design Sun:** Gate 13 — The Listener (Fellowship of Man)
- **Design Earth:** Gate 7 — The Army (Role of the Self)
- **Quarter:** Quarter of Civilization (Realm of Duhbe, Purpose of Form)
- **Angle:** Right Angle — Personal Destiny

The consciousness axis 2–1 is the directional engine: receptive form holding pure creative spirit, the marriage of yin and yang at the leading edge of the Quarter of Form. The unconscious axis 13–7 underwrites it with collective listening and recognisable role. The carrier consciously drives a direction; unconsciously they have been built by every story they have ever quietly absorbed.

## The Four Gates

### Gate 2 (Personality Sun) — The Receptive / Higher Knowing

The conscious purpose of this life is to know where the form needs to go. Gate 2 does not arrive at direction by thinking. It senses it. Ra called this the driver of the magnetic monopole — the part of the self that already knows the direction before the mind catches up. For Sphinx 2, that knowing is the engine of an entire life.

### Gate 1 (Personality Earth) — The Creative / Self-Expression

The grounding of the cross. Beneath the directional sense, there is an originating creative impulse. Sphinx 2 does not just point; they express. The direction they sense surfaces as unmistakable individual expression — a voice, an aesthetic, a creative line that becomes culturally legible.

### Gate 13 (Design Sun) — The Fellowship of Man

The unconscious carrier of the listener. Beneath the surface, Sphinx 2 is still absorbing the human archive. The direction they consciously hold is informed by an entire lifetime of unconscious witness. The body has heard things the mind has not catalogued.

### Gate 7 (Design Earth) — The Army / Role of the Self

Underlies the cross with the role of the self in collective interaction. Sphinx 2 carries an unconscious blueprint for leadership through recognised role — not the noisy leadership of force, but the leadership of someone whose form everyone instinctively follows.

## The Quarter Theme

The Quarter of Civilization is the quarter of form. Where Quarter 1 initiated through mind, Quarter 2 builds through bodies in space — settlements, structures, materials, beauty. Every cross in this quarter contributes to the building of form. Sphinx 2 opens the quarter by giving form its direction. Without the Receptive knowing where to go, there is no civilization to build.

This is why Ra Uru Hu placed such weight on Gate 2 in the Quarter of Civilization: it is the body's pre-cognitive sense of beauty, of placement, of where the form must move. Sphinx 2 carriers are the people who walked into an empty plain and said "the village goes here," not because they reasoned it out but because the form told them.

## The Angle Expression

The Right Angle is the personal destiny — the carrier's own life is the work. For Sphinx 2 this means the direction must first be lived inside the carrier's own body before it can become useful to anyone else. The not-self trap is to start directing others before the carrier has honoured their own internal compass. The mind, hearing the directional gift, jumps to instruct the world; the body, however, is the only thing that actually knows.

A correct Sphinx 2 spends a lifetime learning to trust their own form. The trust is built moment by moment — the carrier finally moving because the body said so, finally staying because the body said so, finally turning down opportunities because the body said no. Once the direction is honoured in the carrier's own life, the same direction begins to magnetise others into the carrier's field without effort.

## Profiles This Cross Manifests In

The Right Angle of any cross expresses through the six classical right-angle profile combinations: 1/3, 1/4, 2/4, 2/5, 3/5, 3/6. For Sphinx 2 the profile flavours the directional work:

- **1/3** investigates direction through study and then by trial-and-error tests the route
- **1/4** investigates direction through deep relationship; the form senses through the network
- **2/4** is the natural hermit whose direction emerges in solitude and is recognised by close friends
- **2/5** carries the called hermit whose direction is projected onto by strangers
- **3/5** mutates direction through trial-and-error in front of an audience
- **3/6** moves through the three life phases — experimenter, hermit-on-the-roof, role model — letting direction mature with age

## Living This Cross

[[Strategy]] and [[Inner Authority]] govern correct movement for Sphinx 2 as for any cross. The directional gift is only safe to follow when filtered through the carrier's authority — sacral, emotional, splenic, ego, self-projected, mental or lunar. The mind cannot manage this gate; it can only watch the body do its work.

The not-self distortion is rushed direction. The mind, sensing that the body knows something, panics and tries to articulate the answer before the body has spoken. The result is a life of false starts — moves that didn't take, decisions reversed, energy spent in directions the form did not actually want. The remedy is the same as it always is: wait through one's strategy, decide through one's authority, let the form move when the form moves.

When honoured, this cross produces what Ra called the natural placement of civilization. The carrier becomes someone whose decisions over a long life — where to live, who to marry, what to build — turn out, in retrospect, to have been quietly, exactly right.
