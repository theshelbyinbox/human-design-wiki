# Incarnation Cross Overview

The Incarnation Cross in [[Human Design System]] is the overarching life theme that emerges from the four gates formed by the Personality Sun, Personality Earth, Design Sun, and Design Earth at birth. These four gates form a cross pattern in the Rave Mandala and give a person's incarnation its general purpose, direction, and thematic backdrop.

## The Four Gates of the Cross

The cross is composed of four gate positions:

1. **Personality Sun**: The conscious personality gate. The most prominent gate in the chart, representing approximately 70 percent of the personality imprint.
2. **Personality Earth**: The grounding counterpart of the Personality Sun. Always the gate directly opposite in the Rave wheel.
3. **Design Sun**: The unconscious design gate. The primary imprint of the body/vehicle consciousness.
4. **Design Earth**: The grounding counterpart of the Design Sun. Always opposite the Design Sun in the wheel.

Because the Sun and Earth are always in opposition (180 degrees apart), each arm of the cross is a straight line. The angle between the two arms -- the Personality arm and the Design arm -- is approximately 88 degrees, not exactly 90. This slight deviation is what creates the distinction between Right Angle and Left Angle crosses.

## Cross Types

There are three types of crosses based on the 88-degree relationship:

- **Right Angle Crosses**: Personal destiny. The majority of crosses.
- **Left Angle Crosses**: Transpersonal karma. A smaller group.
- **Juxtaposition Crosses**: Fixed fate. Each gate has one Juxtaposition cross, giving 64 total.

The same set of four gates can appear as a Right Angle, Left Angle, or Juxtaposition cross depending on which line of the gate the Personality Sun occupies.

## The Four Quarters

The 64 gates of the Rave Mandala are organized into four quarters of 16 gates each. The quarter in which the Personality Sun falls at birth gives the cross its quarterly theme:

- **Quarter of Initiation (Realm of Alcyone)**: Purpose through Mind. Gates 13, 49, 30, 55, 37, 63, 22, 36, 25, 17, 21, 51, 42, 3, 27, 24.
- **Quarter of Civilization (Realm of Duhbe)**: Purpose through Form. Gates beginning with the Sphinx gate and running through manifestation-related themes.
- **Quarter of Duality (Realm of Jupiter)**: Purpose through Bonding. Intimacy, fertility, relationship, and the sustaining of human connection.
- **Quarter of Mutation (Realm of the Godhead)**: Purpose through Transformation. Gates related to mutation, change, and evolutionary shifts.

## Reading the Cross

An Incarnation Cross is identified by the position of the Personality Sun gate and line. For example, the Cross of the Sphinx is identified by having the Personality Sun in Gate 13 (in specific lines). Each gate can generate up to twelve different cross names depending on the line position and whether it is Right Angle, Left Angle, or Juxtaposition.

"Each person in life has a very specific purpose. When one lives who they are by honoring their specific strategy, that purpose is fulfilled." - Ra Uru Hu (from the Global Incarnation Cross Index)

## The Cross Is Not Personal

Ra Uru Hu consistently emphasized that incarnation crosses should not be interpreted personally. They are not identities or personality descriptions -- they are impersonal evolutionary assignments. Each cross is like being a specific type of cell in a larger organism. No cross is more important or special than any other.

The cross theme is most fully lived out when a person is operating from their Strategy and Authority. The cross expresses itself naturally through correct living; it does not need to be consciously pursued or performed.

## Key Named Crosses

The system includes hundreds of named crosses. A few prominent examples:

- **Cross of the Sphinx**: One of the foundational crosses, involving the G Center gates (10, 15, 25, 46).
- **Cross of the Vessel of Love**: The G Center love gates.
- **Cross of the Sleeping Phoenix**: Involves Gate 55, associated with the coming emotional mutation.
- **Cross of the Four Ways**: A foundational cross present in each quarter.
- **Cross of Life**: Gate 60, associated with limitation and the structure within which mutation occurs.
