# Left Angle Cross of Tension 2

The Left Angle Cross of Tension 2 — also called the Left Angle Cross of Individualism 1 in Ra Uru Hu's teaching — is the transpersonal variant of the four-armed Cross of Tension in the Quarter of Civilization. The same four gates — 21, 48, 38, 39 — are present, but the angle is Left rather than Right, and the carrier's life is woven into a transpersonal field of relationships rather than into a private destiny.

Ra spoke of the Left Angle Cross of Individualism with a particular tenderness — he described having a deep attachment to it. The configuration belongs to those whose individualism is not eccentricity but vocation: the people whose departure from the collective pattern is itself the gift they bring to civilization.

## Mechanics

- **Personality Sun:** Gate 39 — Obstruction / The Provocateur (in the Left Angle rotation of the Tension cross)
- **Personality Earth:** Gate 38 — Opposition / The Fighter
- **Design Sun:** Gate 51 — Shock / The Arousing (read by Ra in the Left Angle Individualism column alongside the Tension 2 placement; in some readings the Design axis remains the Tension axis of 21/48)
- **Design Earth:** Gate 57 — The Gentle / Intuition (alternate Left Angle pairing); canonical Tension cross Design axis is 21↔48
- **Quarter:** Quarter of Civilization (Realm of Duhbe, Purpose of Form)
- **Angle:** Left Angle — Transpersonal Destiny

For the canonical Tension cross axis layout in the Left Angle 2 variant, the Personality axis remains 39↔38 and the Design axis remains 21↔48. The carrier's conscious individual root pressure is the front face of the life; the unconscious tribal mechanism is the body underneath.

## The Four Gates

### Gate 39 (Personality Sun) — Obstruction, The Provocateur

The 39 in the Left Angle becomes the provocateur of a karmic field rather than a personal one. The carrier's mood, their teasing, their pressure on the spirit of others, is part of a transpersonal task. They are here to provoke the people they were destined to encounter, not the people they happen to live with.

### Gate 38 (Personality Earth) — Opposition, The Fighter

Gate 38 grounds the provocateur in the fighter for purpose. The Left Angle's fight is for the field, the network, the collective the carrier is karmically connected to.

### Gate 21 (Design Sun) — Control, The Hunter

The unconscious tribal controller. The body's foundation is the right to control material resources. Beneath the conscious provocation, the carrier holds the structural mechanism of organizing the tribal world.

### Gate 48 (Design Earth) — The Well, Depth

The unconscious well. The vehicle carries the qualitative foundation the conscious provocation is built on. The Left Angle Tension 2 carrier provokes consciously and is depth unconsciously.

## The Quarter Theme

The Quarter of Civilization is the realm of form. The Left Angle Tension 2 contributes to form through individualism — the carrier whose departure from collective norms is itself the engine of civilization's mutation. Ra was clear that civilization needs its individualists. Without them, the form crystallizes into stagnation.

## The Angle Expression

A Left Angle Cross is a cross of transpersonal destiny. The Left Angle Tension 2 carrier is here to participate in a karmic field, and the four-armed tension is the configuration through which their participation takes shape. Their provocation, their fight, their depth, and their control are not for their own life — they are for the field they belong to.

The not-self distortion is the individualist who has retreated into private eccentricity. The provocation becomes solipsism. The fight becomes self-righteousness. The depth becomes inwardness with no audience. The control becomes a personal closed system. The correct expression is the opposite: the individualism is given to the field, and the field is changed because of it.

## Profiles This Cross Manifests In

Left Angle Crosses manifest through 4/6, 5/1, 5/2, 6/2, and 6/3.

- **4/6** carries the externalizing individualist — the network is the audience, the role-modeling is the gift
- **5/1** carries the universalizing individualist whose foundation studies make the heresy land
- **5/2** carries the heretical hermit individualist — natural withdrawal meets called provocation
- **6/2** carries the matured hermit individualist whose three life phases reveal an unmistakable signature
- **6/3** carries the experimenter-then-role-model individualist whose trial-and-error becomes the wisdom of an elder

## Living This Cross

[[Strategy]] and [[Inner Authority]] are essential. The 39 in the conscious sun, especially with an emotional carrier, must ride the wave to clarity before the provocation lands. The mind has no jurisdiction over the emotional wave or the splenic fear of uselessness in the 38.

The signature of the cross lived correctly is a field that is structurally different because the carrier was an individualist within it. The signature of the not-self is the carrier who has internalized the four-armed pressure and become merely difficult, merely moody, merely angry, with no field of participation to show for it. The work is to let the individualism be given.

When the carrier honors the design, they become what Ra honored most in this cross: the person whose departure from the collective norm makes the collective norm visible and therefore changeable. They are the mutative pressure of their karmic field. The 39 provokes its spirit. The 38 fights for its purpose. The 21 controls its resources. The 48 holds its depth. The field is altered because they refused to be ordinary in it.

## Individualism in the Quarter of Civilization

Civilization is built on collective norms — the agreed forms that allow human beings to coordinate at scale. The 12, the 22, the 11, the 45, the 17, the 18, the 52, the 58: these are the gates that build the collective container. Without them, no civilization. But civilization also requires the individualist — the person whose departure from the collective form is the mutative pressure that keeps the form from crystallizing into stagnation. The Left Angle Cross of Individualism 1 is one of the configurations that delivers this pressure.

The Tension 2 axis of the cross — 39 conscious, 38 grounded, 21 driving, 48 deep — concentrates the four-armed pressure of the Cross of Tension into a karmic individualist's life. The carrier provokes, fights, controls, and holds depth, and all four of these movements are aimed at the karmic field they belong to. Their individualism is not eccentricity. It is structural. The field changes because the carrier was inside it being something the field had not yet learned to accommodate.

Ra spoke of the Cross of Individualism with deep affection in part because he recognized its loneliness. The individualist who is correctly individual is often misread by the collective as a problem. The carrier must therefore develop a tolerance for being misunderstood and a trust in the design that overrides the social pressure to fit. The 38's fight for purpose is the structural support that makes this tolerance possible.
