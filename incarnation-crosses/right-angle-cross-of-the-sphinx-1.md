# Right Angle Cross of the Sphinx 1

The Right Angle Cross of the Sphinx 1 is the opening cross of the entire wheel — the very first incarnation cross in the Quarter of Initiation. Carriers of this configuration arrive at the leading edge of the Quarter of Mind with the Personality Sun in Gate 13, the Listener. Their life is a personal destiny built around becoming the receptive memory-keeper for the human family — the one to whom strangers will tell their stories, the one whose role is to gather what has been lived and let it become guidance for those coming after.

The cross asks a single question across the whole life: what is the past trying to teach the present? The carrier is here to answer it, not by inventing a future, but by listening so deeply to what has already been lived that the future organises itself out of that hearing.

## Mechanics

- **Personality Sun:** Gate 13 — Fellowship of Man (the Listener)
- **Personality Earth:** Gate 7 — The Army (the Role of the Self)
- **Design Sun:** Gate 1 — The Creative (Self-Expression)
- **Design Earth:** Gate 2 — The Receptive (Higher Knowing)
- **Quarter:** Quarter of Initiation (Realm of Alcyone, Purpose of Mind)
- **Angle:** Right Angle — Personal Destiny

The Personality axis 13–7 is the consciousness axis of this life: the listener and the role of the self in interaction. The Design axis 1–2 is the underlying vehicle: pure creative spirit set into pure receptive form. The wheel position of Gate 13 anchors this cross at the very gate that opens the Quarter of Initiation, which is why Ra Uru Hu placed it first in his teaching of the 64 crosses.

## The Four Gates

### Gate 13 (Personality Sun) — The Fellowship of Man

The 13 is the gate of the witness. Carriers of this gate become the natural confessionals of their environment — the ones strangers approach in the supermarket and start telling secrets to without knowing why. The conscious purpose of this cross is to be that witness on a designed schedule, holding the memory of human experience and feeding it back to others when it is asked for.

### Gate 7 (Personality Earth) — The Army / The Role of the Self

Gate 7 carries the role the carrier plays in interaction with others. As the grounding of the 13, it gives the listener a coherent identity to wear in public. The Sphinx 1 carrier is not simply a passive receiver; they have a recognisable role — therapist, archivist, mentor, the one who has heard everything before — that the collective can locate and use.

### Gate 1 (Design Sun) — The Creative / Self-Expression

The unconscious creative engine of the cross. Beneath the listening, there is a pure originating self yearning for unique expression. This is the part of the carrier that wants their gathered understanding to come out as something singular, not merely repeated.

### Gate 2 (Design Earth) — The Receptive / Higher Knowing

The driver of the Magnetic Monopole, the form's intelligent knowing of where it must go. Beneath the conscious role, the body already knows the direction. The carrier's life moves correctly when the form is allowed to lead and the mind does not try to manage the route.

## The Quarter Theme

The Quarter of Initiation is the realm of mind. Its purpose is to begin the journey of human understanding by collecting, codifying and transmitting what has already been lived. Every cross in this quarter contributes to that mental initiation in a different way. The Sphinx 1 sits at the opening of the quarter and gives it its leading direction: to listen to the past so the present can begin.

This is the cross of the storyteller as historian, the elder as repository, the friend who has heard every confession on the block. The mind initiated through this cross is one that learns by listening rather than by experimenting. The carrier's contribution to the wheel is to hold the human archive open.

## The Angle Expression

A Right Angle Cross is a cross of personal destiny. The life is the carrier's own — their secrets to uncover, their mistakes to make, their meaning to assemble from their own hours. Ra Uru Hu put it plainly: a Right Angle is not about finding somebody else's secret. It is about doing the work of becoming who one was designed to be.

For Sphinx 1, this means the carrier does not exist to fix the people who confess to them. They are not here as a service-counter for other people's lives. They are here to listen because in the listening their own identity assembles itself. The stories they collect become the raw material of their own direction.

The not-self distortion is the carrier who believes the listener role obliges them to give advice, intervene, or carry the emotional load of every story heard. That is the mind hijacking a body-level design. The correct Sphinx 1 listens, witnesses, and lets the gathered material settle into wisdom that informs their own life first.

## Profiles This Cross Manifests In

The Right Angle of any cross is carried by the six classical right-angle profiles: 1/3, 1/4, 2/4, 2/5, 3/5 and 3/6. Each profile flavours the Sphinx 1 differently:

- **1/3** carries the investigator who studies the secrets and then learns by trial which secrets are real
- **1/4** carries the investigator whose listening network is built through close friends
- **2/4** carries the natural hermit whose friendships become the channel through which their gift is recognised
- **2/5** carries the called hermit whose gift is projected onto by strangers seeking a saviour
- **3/5** carries the trial-and-error mutator whose listening becomes practical wisdom
- **3/6** carries the role-modelling experimenter who moves through the three life phases as a witness in maturation

## Living This Cross

[[Strategy]] and [[Inner Authority]] are the only reliable navigation system for this configuration. The carrier's [[Type]] determines how the listener role lands correctly: a Projector with this cross waits for invitation to be recognised as the witness; a Generator with this cross responds with the sacral when invited to listen; a Manifestor informs before the listening cuts across someone's life; a Reflector samples the gathering and lets a lunar cycle reveal whose stories were worth keeping.

The signature of the cross lived well is a steady accumulation of meaning. The not-self signature is heaviness — the burden of carrying everyone else's pain because the mind decided the body had to absorb it. The work is to listen without absorbing, to remember without re-living, to let the stories become wisdom rather than weight.

When the carrier honours the design, they become what Ra called the natural counsellor of humanity. People feel safer in their presence. They tell the truth without rehearsing it. And the carrier, in turn, finds themselves becoming the person their own listening has revealed them to be.
