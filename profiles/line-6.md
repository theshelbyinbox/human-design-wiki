# Line 6 — The Role Model

The sixth line is the top of the hexagram, the final line, the line of completion. It is the only line in the system with a built-in developmental arc — three distinct life phases that unfold over decades rather than a single mode of being. In [[Human Design System]], the Role Model is the archetype that earns its authority through the full cycle of experience, not through theory, and that eventually offers something transpersonal back to the collective.

The sixth line is sometimes called "the line of the roof." In the house metaphor that traditional Human Design uses for the lines, the sixth line is the person up on the roof of the house, looking out over everything. From that height, they see what the people inside the house cannot see. The sixth line's authority is the authority of long perspective.

## Position in the Hexagram

Line 6 sits at the very top of the hexagram, the apex of the upper trigram. The upper trigram (Lines 4, 5, 6) is the transpersonal half of the human experience, and Line 6 is its culmination — the line where transpersonal awareness fully matures. Above the Role Model there is nothing in the hexagram. The sixth line is the line that has, in some sense, exited the structure entirely. It is on the roof.

Line 6 has a harmonic relationship with [[Line 3]]. Both lines learn through trial and error. Line 3 is in the trial-and-error process actively, life-long. Line 6 lives through a tripartite arc in which the first thirty years are themselves a third-line phase — full-immersion experimentation — before the line moves into its later modes. This is the 3-6 harmonic, and it shows up most directly in the 3/6 and 6/3 profiles, where both numbers carry experiential discovery as the dominant dynamic. The third line carries the encounter; the sixth line carries the eventual integration.

## The Archetype

The Role Model is the one whose life itself is the teaching. They are not here to give a lecture. They are here to live long enough, deeply enough, and visibly enough that other people learn by watching them. The sixth line's wisdom does not come from study or from being called out. It comes from living through the three phases and arriving on the other side.

The three phases of the sixth-line life:

**Phase 1 — birth to around 30: The third-line phase.** The young sixth line is structurally living as a third line. Trial and error. Encounter and breakdown. Bonds made and bonds dissolved. This phase can be intense, sometimes painful, often confusing — and crucially, it is supposed to be. The sixth line is gathering the raw material that the later phases will integrate. A sixth line who tries to skip this phase, who tries to live as the wise observer at age 22, has nothing to actually observe. The third-line phase is the input layer of the sixth-line life.

**Phase 2 — roughly 30 to 50: On the roof.** Around the first Saturn return, the sixth line withdraws. They detach from the drama of full engagement. They climb up onto the roof of the house and begin to observe rather than immerse. This phase can feel like a deliberate quieting down — less interest in the same intensities, more interest in pattern, in distance, in seeing the whole picture. It is sometimes experienced as healing, sometimes as exile, sometimes as both. The sixth line is integrating the lessons of phase 1.

**Phase 3 — roughly 50 onward: The Role Model.** Around the Chiron return, the sixth line re-engages, but at a different altitude. They come down from the roof — not into the same kind of immersion they had at 25, but into the embodied authority of someone who has lived through it and watched it and now has something genuinely transpersonal to offer. People around them recognize this without being told. The sixth line in phase 3 is the Role Model that the line's name actually describes.

## What This Line Brings

The Role Model brings earned wisdom. When the sixth line is operating correctly through [[Strategy]] and [[Inner Authority]], they become a transpersonal authority whose presence teaches more than any of their explicit statements. People look at them and adjust their own behavior, not because they were told to, but because the example is visible.

The sixth line also brings the long view. Where most lines are in the situation, the sixth line — particularly in the roof phase and beyond — sees the situation. They notice patterns that the people inside the patterns cannot notice. This perspective is itself a contribution to any collective they are part of.

There is also a quality of trustworthiness to the integrated sixth line. They have been through it. They know what works because they have watched it work and seen what does not. Other people sense this and bring real questions to them, knowing the answers will be grounded.

## The Shadow / Not-Self Expression

The shadow of the sixth line shows up most often as premature withdrawal or premature authority. The young sixth line who withdraws to the roof too early — at 19 instead of 32 — has nothing to observe and ends up isolated rather than integrating. The young sixth line who tries to be the wise role model before they have lived the third-line phase comes across as preachy, distant, or out of touch.

Under [[Conditioning]], the sixth line can also become disillusioned. The trial-and-error of phase 1 can land as cynicism if the design is not understood. "I have already seen it all and none of it is real" is a [[Not-Self]] story for this line. The cure is not more distance but proper unfolding through the phases.

The phase 2 roof period can also be hard for a sixth line who does not understand what is happening. The withdrawal can feel like depression, like exile, like something is wrong with them. In a culture that valorizes constant engagement, sitting on the roof looks like failure. Understanding the design lets the sixth line trust the phase rather than fighting it.

A sixth line who has not yet entered the role-model phase can also be impatient with their own life — feeling that they should already be embodying the wisdom they sense is coming. The integration takes time. The Saturn return and Chiron return are real markers, and the line is not meant to skip them.

## In Profile

The sixth line appears as the conscious (Personality) line in two profiles: [[Profile 6/2]] and [[Profile 6/3]]. In both, the person consciously identifies with the Role Model — they recognize the tripartite arc, the pull toward observation, the sense that they are moving toward something they cannot fully name yet.

The sixth line appears as the unconscious (Design) line in two profiles: [[Profile 3/6]] and [[Profile 4/6]]. In these profiles the Role Model is running in the body — the developmental arc is operating underneath a conscious Personality that identifies with experimentation (3/6) or with externalized network (4/6). The roof-phase pull is still there, often as a deepening need for distance that the personality may not fully claim.

## In Every Gate

All 64 gates in the BodyGraph contain six lines. The sixth line of any gate carries the gate's content in its transpersonal, completed form. Where the first line of a gate is investigative and inward, the sixth line is the same gift expressed as embodied authority — the gate's content fully integrated and offered as example rather than as effort.

When someone has a Line 6 activation in a specific gate, that gate's gift tends to express through the role-model arc — early experimentation, mid-life observation, later transpersonal embodiment. The line is a meta-layer that colors every gate it touches with the quality of long perspective.

## Living This Line

Living the sixth line well begins with trusting the phases. The third-line years at the beginning are supposed to be intense. The roof years in the middle are supposed to be quiet. The role-model years at the end are supposed to be the harvest of everything that came before. None of these phases is a mistake. [[Strategy]] and [[Inner Authority]] are how the sixth line moves through each phase correctly rather than skipping over it.

Practically, the sixth line benefits from honoring the phase they are actually in. The 25-year-old sixth line is allowed to be in the middle of life and bumping into things. The 40-year-old sixth line is allowed to want distance and quiet observation. The 55-year-old sixth line is allowed to re-engage as someone whose authority comes from lived experience rather than performance.

The sixth line also benefits from accepting that they are watched. People look at the sixth line. Even in the roof phase, when the sixth line believes they are off-stage, the example continues to land. This is part of the design. The Role Model is not optional. The only choice is whether the example being modeled is the Not-Self pattern or the line's correct expression.
