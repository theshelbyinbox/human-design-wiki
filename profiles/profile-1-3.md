# Profile 1/3 - Investigator/Martyr

The 1/3 profile is the first of the twelve profiles in [[Human Design System]], beginning the chain of Right Angle personal destiny profiles. It combines a first-line Personality (the Investigator) with a third-line Design (the Martyr). Both lines belong to the lower trigram, making the 1/3 the most self-absorbed of all profiles -- the most completely oriented toward personal process.

## The Investigator Personality (Line 1)

The first-line personality is driven by the need for a solid foundation. Investigation is not merely intellectual curiosity -- it is a response to an underlying fear that the foundation may not be stable. The 1/3 person's mind continuously seeks security through knowledge, research, and thorough understanding before moving forward.

Line 1 personalities are not naturally social in their orientation. They dig inward, probing for truth, verifying facts, building the internal structure that allows them to feel secure. They do not appreciate being misled. A 1/3 child told a comforting fiction who later discovers the truth may never trust that person again.

"Everything about the nature of this first profile is that this first profile establishes the real mystery of life. That one personality so much it wants everything to be absolutely stable, reliable, understandable, knowable." - Ra Uru Hu

## The Martyr Design (Line 3)

The third-line design is a trial-and-error body. The 1/3 person's vehicle learns through direct encounter, through bumping into things, through discovering what does not work. The third-line body is resilient and material -- built for the density of the physical plane.

The challenge is that the Investigator personality wants stability while the Martyr body keeps discovering instability. The personality wants to build a secure foundation while the body keeps experiencing what shatters foundations. This internal tension is the core dynamic of the 1/3.

Third-line people are frequently criticized for their "mistakes." In a homogenized world, the trial-and-error process is often pathologized as failure. The Human Design reframe: the 1/3 is not making mistakes -- they are discovering what doesn't work, which is essential for the collective's understanding.

## The Not-Self Pattern

Without correct Strategy and Authority, the 1/3 can develop deep pessimism (third-line shadow) layered with an inferiority complex (first-line fear). They may internalize their "failures" as evidence of personal inadequacy rather than as the natural output of their discovery function. This combination -- pessimism plus fear of instability -- can create a defensive, rigid personality that closes down rather than investigates.

## Living the 1/3

The 1/3 profile is lived correctly when the person can:
- Trust their investigative nature without shame
- Allow trial-and-error without self-blame
- Recognize that their "mistakes" serve a purpose
- Operate from Strategy and Authority to ensure they bump into the right things

"To be a one-three is so beautiful. There is such an incredible contribution that could be made in the perfection of that personal destiny." - Ra Uru Hu

## Angle

Right Angle. Personal destiny. The 1/3 is here to live out its own process, not to serve a transpersonal mission.
