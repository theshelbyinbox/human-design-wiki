# Profile 3/5 - Martyr/Heretic

The 3/5 profile is a Right Angle profile in [[Human Design System]], combining a third-line Personality (the Martyr) with a fifth-line Design (the Heretic). It is one of the most experientially intense profiles, pairing the trial-and-error discovery process with the high projection field of the fifth line.

## The Martyr Personality (Line 3)

The third-line personality is entirely experiential. The 3/5 person's conscious identity is shaped through direct encounter, trial and error, and the discovery of what does not work. They are resilient, material, and prone to pessimism as a shadow when their errors are consistently criticized rather than celebrated.

The Martyr personality takes the discoveries personally. When something doesn't work, there is a risk of internalizing the failure as personal inadequacy. The reframe: the 3/5's job is to discover what does not work, and every "failed" experiment is a successful data point.

## The Heretic Design (Line 5)

The fifth-line design carries a strong projection field. Others project onto the 3/5, seeing a practical solution-bringer, a universal fixer. This projection often doesn't match the 3/5's self-perception (as the martyr who makes mistakes). The gap between external projection and internal experience can be confusing.

The Heretic aspect means the 3/5 has a universalizing function: the experiential discoveries of the Martyr personality become, through the fifth-line design, broadly applicable lessons for others.

## Practical Pattern

The 3/5 tends to have many varied experiences -- relationships, jobs, places, approaches -- discovering through direct encounter what works and what doesn't. Others often look to them as sources of practical, tested wisdom, even as the 3/5 continues to feel uncertain or provisional in their knowing.

## Angle

Right Angle. Personal destiny, with fifth-line projection effects that serve others as a byproduct.
