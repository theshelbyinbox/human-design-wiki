# Angles Overview

In [[Human Design System]], the twelve profiles are divided into three angles based on their geometric relationship to the cross of incarnation: Right Angle, Juxtaposition, and Left Angle. The angle describes the general quality of a person's destiny -- whether it is personal, fixed, or transpersonal in nature.

## Right Angle: Personal Destiny

Seven of the twelve profiles are Right Angle: 1/3, 1/4, 2/4, 2/5, 3/5, 3/6, and 4/6. The Right Angle represents personal destiny -- a life organized around one's own experience, one's own process, one's own discovery.

"In the way in which they incarnated into the conditions and circumstances of their incarnation, their movement in this life is a movement rooted in their own personal trip." - Ra Uru Hu

Right Angle people are not here to serve a transpersonal mission. They are here to live out their own nature fully. Paradoxically, this personal fulfillment can have enormous ripple effects on others -- but those effects are a byproduct, not the primary purpose.

The great challenge for Right Angle beings is homogenization. A world that imposes uniform paths limits the personal destiny that Right Angle beings need to discover through their own experience.

## Juxtaposition: Fixed Fate

One profile occupies the Juxtaposition position: the 4/1. The Juxtaposition is the bridge between the Right Angle and the Left Angle. It has neither pure personal destiny nor transpersonal karma -- it has fixed fate.

The Juxtaposition person's path is fixed and singular. There is less flexibility, less serendipity, and less of the "anything could happen" quality of Right Angle. The 4/1 person tends to move in one direction, through one life theme, in one consistent way. This fixedness can feel like limitation or like clarity, depending on alignment.

## Left Angle: Transpersonal Karma

Four profiles are Left Angle: 5/1, 5/2, 6/2, and 6/3. The Left Angle represents transpersonal karma -- a life organized around encounters with others, service to the collective, and the playing out of karmic themes that extend beyond the individual.

Left Angle people often feel that their lives are not entirely their own. They are "used" by life in a transpersonal sense -- their encounters with others, their roles in communities, their relationships serve a larger pattern that is not solely about their personal development.

"My job is to spread the mutation. But the mutation arises out of the human experience." - Ra Uru Hu (a 5/1 Left Angle)

The Left Angle person may feel compelled by forces outside their control, drawn into relationships and situations that serve others' evolution as much as their own.

## Calculating the Angle

The angle is determined by the position of the Personality Sun at birth. If the Personality Sun falls in the first through fourth line (approximately), the cross will be Right Angle. If it falls in the fifth or sixth line in a position that shifts the Design Sun out of alignment, it becomes Left Angle. The 4/1 Juxtaposition occupies a specific mathematical position between the two.

The angle does not appear directly in the profile number -- it is determined by the actual degree position of the Personality Sun within the hexagram line.

## Practical Implications

Right Angle people can release themselves from the obligation to "help" or "serve" in a deliberate way. Their job is to live their personal destiny fully. Left Angle people often find their purpose expressed through their encounters with and impact on others, even when they are simply living correctly by their Strategy and Authority.
