# Lines Overview

In [[Human Design System]], the six lines are the fundamental building blocks of the profile system. Every gate in the BodyGraph contains six lines, drawn from the hexagram structure of the Chinese I Ching. Profile is determined by the line numbers of the Personality Sun and Design Sun positions in the chart. The result is always a two-number combination (e.g., 3/5, 1/4) representing the personality line (conscious) and design line (unconscious).

## The Six Lines

### Line 1: The Investigator
The foundation of the hexagram. Line 1 energy is introspective, self-absorbed in the constructive sense, and driven by the need to build a solid knowledge base before moving. Line 1 personalities fear instability and are motivated by the drive to know, to research, and to establish ground truth. They are natural researchers who need to thoroughly investigate before they can feel secure.

"The first line personality wants to dig into things and find out what's really true, what's really solid, what really works." - Ra Uru Hu

Key qualities: investigative, thorough, security-seeking, knowledge-oriented, foundation-building. The line 1 person needs a solid foundation and builds it through study and research.

### Line 2: The Hermit
The natural genius. Line 2 carries innate abilities and gifts that the person may not be fully aware of. The line 2 person needs time alone -- time in their own world, engaged in what they love -- to develop and express their natural gifts. They do not seek recognition but often receive it, called out by life precisely when they have retreated.

Key qualities: naturally gifted, selective, needs solitude, called out, unaware of own genius. The line 2 person is not designed to be "on" continuously; they need retreat to access what they carry.

### Line 3: The Martyr
The experiential researcher. Line 3 learns through direct experience, through trial and error, through bumping into things and discovering what does not work. The third line body is resilient and material -- built to engage with the physical world through encounter and discovery.

"The third line discovers what others cannot see. And yet how blind it can be in a homogenized world when others tell it where to look." - Ra Uru Hu

Key qualities: experimental, resilient, discovery through failure, pessimism as shadow, material plane engagement. Third-line people are not making mistakes -- they are performing the necessary function of finding what does not work so others do not have to.

### Line 4: The Opportunist
The networker and foundation of the upper trigram. Line 4 is the first line of the upper trigram and is oriented toward the other rather than the self. Opportunities come through existing networks and relationships. The fourth line must invest energy in the right people because those people are the medium through which the fourth line's influence flows.

Key qualities: networked, influential, externalized, relationship-oriented. Classic advice for line 4 people: do not leave a position (job, relationship, home) until the next one is secured through the network. Opportunities come from people you know, not from strangers.

### Line 5: The Heretic
The practical and universalizing force. The fifth line carries a strong projection field -- others project onto line 5 people, often seeing in them a savior, a fixer, or a universal solution. The fifth line's gift is the capacity to take what has been established (through lines 1-4) and universalize it -- to apply it broadly, practically, across contexts.

Key qualities: universalizing, projected upon, practical, seen as heretic or savior, magnetic. Fifth line people must be careful about which projections they accept and which situations they step into, because the projection field can attract both genuine recognition and unrealistic expectations.

### Line 6: The Role Model
The line of the roof. The sixth line lives in three distinct phases: years 0-30 (lived as a third line -- trial and error); years 30-50 (on the roof -- observation, integration, withdrawal from the fray); age 50+ (return -- embodying the role model with wisdom gained). The sixth line is the only profile line with a built-in developmental arc.

Key qualities: tripartite life, role model, wisdom through experience, observer phase, return to engagement. The sixth line is here to become an authority through the full cycle of experience, not through theory.

## Lower and Upper Trigrams

Lines 1-3 form the lower trigram and are considered more personal and self-referential in their orientation. Lines 4-6 form the upper trigram and are oriented toward the other and the collective.

## Harmonic Profiles

Six of the twelve profiles are "harmonic" -- their two line numbers have a natural relationship across the trigrams (1/4, 2/5, 3/6, 4/1, 5/2, 6/3). These are also called transitional profiles and represent a smaller percentage of the population than the non-harmonic profiles.
