# Gate 10 — Behavior of the Self (Treading)

Gate 10 is the gate of behavior in the G Center. It is the place in the BodyGraph where the question is not what you do but how you carry yourself while you are doing it — the way you tread on the ground of your own life. Ra Uru Hu called Gate 10 one of his favorite gates, and it is no accident: it sits at the heart of the Integration circuit, surrounded by Gates 20, 34, and 57, and it is the gate most directly concerned with the love of being oneself. Where most gates of the G Center are about direction or love, Gate 10 is about the lived-in form that love takes. Behavior is the visible expression of identity.

The hexagram is Treading, which the I Ching depicts as treading on the tail of a tiger. The image is of a careful, alive way of moving through danger — neither cowering nor showy, just present and correct. In the BodyGraph the tiger is the form itself, the body that must be loved and respected for life to be lived well. Gate 10 carries the instruction set for that respect.

## Mechanics

Gate 10 sits in the [[G Center]] and is one of only four gates in the BodyGraph that belong to the [[Integration Channels]] — the cluster of self-empowerment gates concerned with individual survival. It is unusual in that it can form three different channels, each one connecting to a different part of the Integration cluster:

- [[Channel 10/20]], the Channel of Awakening, connecting the G Center to the [[Throat Center]]
- [[Channel 10/34]], the Channel of Exploration, connecting the G Center to the [[Sacral Center]]
- [[Channel 10/57]], the Channel of Perfected Form, connecting the G Center to the [[Splenic Center]]

This three-way potential is what makes Gate 10 structurally central. It is the only G Center gate that has multiple Integration channel partners, and each partnership expresses a different facet of self-love. With Gate 20, Gate 10 becomes awakening — being conscious in the moment of your own behavior. With Gate 34, it becomes the conviction to follow your own difference. With Gate 57, it becomes the survival instinct refined into perfected form.

Gate 10 belongs to the quarter of Mutation in Ra's quarters teaching — the quarter whose theme is the love of being different. The gate is also part of the cross of the Vessel of Love, which divides the wheel into four and orients each gate of the G Center toward a particular flavor of love. Gate 10's flavor is the love of self, which Ra positioned as the foundational love from which all other loves become possible.

## The Hexagram

Treading describes the conduct of someone walking carefully on the tail of a tiger without being eaten. The wisdom is that correct behavior in dangerous circumstances arises not from cleverness but from a deep, embodied respect for the situation. Applied to the Self, the tiger is one's own form — its needs, its rhythms, its difference from others — and the correct treading is to live in such a way that the form is honored rather than overridden.

## What This Gate Brings

Gate 10 brings the gift of authentic behavior. When the person with this gate is operating through Strategy and Authority, their conduct in the world is unforced and recognizable as their own. They do not perform identity; they live it. The result is what Ra called the natural example — the everyday demonstration of how to be oneself.

Ra emphasized that the example produced by Gate 10 is not the kind that becomes famous. "Not the example that gets glorified on TV, but the everyday example — the example not of how to become famous, not of how to become this or that, but the example as to how to be you." — Ra Uru Hu. Gate 10 produces role models in the most ordinary sense of the term: people whose presence reminds others that authenticity is available.

For people with Gate 10 defined, the underlying experience is a continual return to self. They love themselves not as an idea but as a body, a behavior, a rhythm. That love becomes contagious in the auras around them. People near a correctly operating Gate 10 tend to find their own permission to be different.

## The Shadow / Not-Self Expression

The shadow of Gate 10 is self-rejection. When the mind takes over the question of behavior, the person tries to figure out what the correct conduct ought to be — what would please others, what would fit in, what would avoid criticism. The result is homogenization: blending into the crowd at the cost of one's actual difference. Ra was direct about how painful this is. "It's difficult for me to find a negative in that gate, you know, other than hating oneself." — Ra Uru Hu.

The not-self mind in Gate 10 produces self-judgment, self-loathing, and a chronic sense of having to perform a version of oneself that is acceptable to others. The deeper the not-self runs, the further the person drifts from their actual behavior, until even small choices feel like negotiations with an internal critic. The correction, as always, is Strategy and Authority — but for Gate 10 specifically, the correction also includes the willingness to be different even when difference is uncomfortable.

## The Six Lines

### Line 1 — Modesty

The 1st line of Gate 10 expresses behavior through humility and discretion. It does not announce itself. In the not-self it can become self-effacing to the point of disappearing. In its gift, it is behavior that does not need recognition to be authentic.

### Line 2 — The Hermit

The 2nd line withdraws to find correct behavior. It needs solitude to know what its own conduct is, separate from the influence of others. In its gift, it is the natural Self that emerges when no one is watching.

### Line 3 — The Martyr

The 3rd line learns correct behavior by behaving incorrectly and discovering the consequences. It is the experimental line, and in Gate 10 it experiments with the limits of acceptable conduct. In its gift, it produces resilience and embodied wisdom about what works.

### Line 4 — The Opportunist

The 4th line is socially attuned behavior. It knows how to read a room. In the not-self it can become chameleonic, shifting behavior to match each new context. In its gift, it is the line that brings authentic self-expression into community without losing itself.

### Line 5 — The Heretic

The 5th line carries behavior that breaks the rules in service of something deeper. It is the line of the person who tread on the tiger's tail and lived. In its gift, the heretic models a way of being that others recognize as truer than the rules they had been following.

### Line 6 — The Role Model

The 6th line is the line of the example itself. It is behavior so fully integrated that other people learn from simply watching. In its gift, it is Gate 10's purest expression: the lived demonstration of self-love.

## In the BodyGraph

Gate 10 alone, without one of its three channel partners, is a hanging gate of behavior. The person carries the love of self but waits for the matching gate to complete one of the Integration channels through electromagnetic connection. Each completion produces a different experience: meeting Gate 20 brings the awakening of self-expression, meeting Gate 34 brings the empowerment of individual conviction, meeting Gate 57 brings the refinement of survival instinct into form.

When Gate 10 is in full channel definition, the person carries a self-empowerment mechanism that operates independent of context. They are who they are wherever they go. This is rare and powerful, and it tends to mark the person as someone who simply cannot be homogenized. Across types, Gate 10 in a [[Generator]] design grounds Sacral response in self-love; in a [[Projector]] design it sharpens the recognition of others' authenticity; in a [[Manifestor]] design it gives the initiator a clear behavioral signature.
