# Mental Authority

Mental Authority (also called No Inner Authority, or Outer Authority) is the decision-making framework for Mental Projectors -- [[Projector]] types whose definition exists only in the head and ajna centers (and possibly the throat) but not in any of the body's motor or awareness centers below the throat.

## Core Feature: No Inner Authority

Persons with Mental Authority have no consistent inner authority to consult. Their body carries no fixed wisdom center that can reliably signal yes or no. This is not a deficiency -- it means their decision-making is designed to be relational and environmental rather than internal.

"For the Mental Projector, there is no inner authority. The authority is outside." - Ra Uru Hu

## How Decisions Are Made

Mental Projectors make decisions by:

- **Talking through decisions with multiple people**: Not for advice, but to hear the quality and resonance of different conversations. Different people will reflect different aspects back.
- **Attending to environment**: Noticing how they feel in different physical spaces. The right environment produces clarity; the wrong one produces confusion.
- **Observing over time**: Allowing the input of multiple perspectives and environments to settle. Mental Projectors are not designed to decide immediately.

The decision-making process is the process of sampling input from outside, then noticing what feels aligned across those samples.

## Outer Authority

Despite having no inner authority, Mental Projectors often serve as exceptionally clear outer authorities for others. Their minds, unburdened by body-based agendas, can see situations clearly and offer guidance that is genuinely useful. The mental projector's gift is awareness and perspective -- but this clarity applies to others, not to themselves. For themselves, they need the outside world.
