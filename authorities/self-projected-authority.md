# Self-Projected Authority

Self-Projected Authority is an inner decision-making mechanism available to some [[Projector]] types who have definition connecting the [[G Center]] to the [[Throat Center]] without definition in the [[Solar Plexus Center]], [[Sacral Center]], [[Splenic Center]], or [[Heart Center]].

## How It Works

For people with Self-Projected Authority, the voice carries the authority. Clarity about a decision arrives by talking it through out loud with another person. The person is not seeking advice -- they are using the listener as a sounding board while they hear what they themselves say.

The clarity is in the words that come out, not in the analysis that preceded them. A person with this authority may not know what they want until they hear themselves say it. The right words tend to feel grounded, clear, and true. Words that arise from conditioning tend to feel hollow or uncertain when spoken aloud.

## The Role of the Listener

The listener's role is to be present without advising. The best support for someone using Self-Projected Authority is to ask open questions and listen without redirecting. The moment the listener begins to counsel, analyze, or project their own preferences onto the speaker, the authority process is disrupted.

This makes Self-Projected Authority unusual: it requires social interaction but is not social in outcome. The decision belongs entirely to the speaker.

## Practical Application

People with Self-Projected Authority may benefit from journaling aloud (speaking while writing), having a trusted person available to listen regularly, or recording themselves speaking about decisions and then listening back. The pattern of which statements feel true vs. which feel performed becomes discernible with practice.
