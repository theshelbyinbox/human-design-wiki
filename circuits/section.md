# Circuits

A circuit in Human Design is a family of channels and gates that carry a shared energetic purpose. The BodyGraph is not a flat map of nine centers and thirty-six channels; it is wired into distinct groupings — each with its own frequency, its own keynote, and its own way of moving through the body. There are three main circuit groups: Individual, Tribal, and Collective. Two minor sub-circuits — Integration and Defense — sit inside the larger groupings and carry specialized functions. Every channel, every gate, and every motor-to-throat pathway belongs somewhere in this architecture, and the circuit a channel sits in tells you who that energy is for and how it is meant to operate.

The three main circuits answer three different questions. Individual asks, *what is true and right for me*. Tribal asks, *what does my group of people need to survive and stay bonded*. Collective asks, *what pattern can be shared with everyone*. Knowing which circuit a defined channel sits in is the simplest way to understand what that channel is doing in a chart — whether it carries mutative knowing that must wait for the right moment, support energy that operates only inside binding agreements, or pattern recognition that wants to be communicated broadly. The five circuits below cover all 36 channels in the BodyGraph.

The three groups also operate on different timeframes and serve different audiences. Individual energy pulses on and off and serves the singular self. Tribal energy runs in cycles of need and provision and serves a bounded group of people in direct relationship. Collective energy runs in long arcs of pattern recognition and serves the species at large. The sub-circuits — Integration inside Individual, Ego inside Tribal — are not separate circuits in their own right; they are specialized concentrations within the larger groupings. Integration is the densest internal wiring of the Individual force, built from only four gates that form six channels. Ego is the willpower stream of the tribe, every one of its channels routing through the Heart Center.

A useful way to read any chart is to count which circuits are activated. Heavy Individual definition tells you the person is here to carry something unique that the tribe and the collective cannot produce. Heavy Tribal definition tells you the person is built for the material life of family and community. Heavy Collective definition tells you the person is here to test patterns for the future or recall and shape meaning from the past. Most charts have channels in more than one circuit, and the interplay between them is where the lived complexity of a design shows up.
